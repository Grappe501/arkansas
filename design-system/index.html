Timeline Card

1907

Tillman Act

First federal ban on corporate contributions to federal campaigns — the beginning of modern campaign finance law.

Case Summary

Supreme Court · 2010

Citizens United v. FEC

The Court held that independent political spending by corporations and unions is protected speech under the First Amendment.

Constitutional Concept

Political Speech

Speech about candidates and elections receives strong First Amendment protection — a central issue in Citizens United.

Fact Callout

Documented change

Independent expenditures in federal elections rose sharply after 2010. Always distinguish documented trends from interpretation.

Myth vs. Fact

Myth: Citizens United allowed unlimited donations directly to candidates.
Fact: The case concerned independent expenditures — spending not coordinated with candidates.

Pull Quote

"The First Amendment does not permit Congress to make categorical distinctions based on the speaker's identity." — Citizens United v. FEC (2010)
