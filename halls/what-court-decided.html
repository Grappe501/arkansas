  • Corporate independent expenditures
  • Disclosure requirements
  • 8-1 on disclosure; 5-4 on spending limits

The Court struck down limits on independent political spending by corporations and unions. The majority said such spending is protected speech. The government may still require disclosure of who paid for ads. Direct contributions to candidates remain regulable. The decision did not say corporations are people — it said restrictions on independent political spending violate the First Amendment.
