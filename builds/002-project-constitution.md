# Citizens United Education Platform

## Project Constitution v1.0

---

# 1. Purpose

The Citizens United Education Platform exists to create the most comprehensive public education resource ever built about *Citizens United v. Federal Election Commission*.

Its purpose is to educate—not merely inform—by taking visitors from little or no knowledge to a deep understanding of the case, its historical context, its constitutional foundations, its measurable impacts, and the range of proposed reforms.

Education is the primary mission.

Leadership development is the ultimate outcome.

---

# 2. Vision

We believe an informed public is essential to a healthy representative democracy.

Complex legal decisions should not be understandable only to attorneys, judges, or academics. Every citizen should have access to clear, accurate, evidence-based explanations that allow them to reach their own informed conclusions.

This platform will become the definitive educational resource dedicated exclusively to understanding *Citizens United*.

---

# 3. Guiding Philosophy

The platform is built around one principle:

**People learn in layers.**

Readers should never be forced into overwhelming detail before they understand the basics.

Every subject should begin with simple explanations and allow progressively deeper exploration through structured learning paths.

Learning is self-directed.

Curiosity determines depth.

---

# 4. Core Reader Journey

Every experience within the platform should support movement through the following progression:

**Discover** → **Understand** → **Explore** → **Evaluate** → **Discuss** → **Teach** → **Lead**

---

# 5. Project Principles

The platform shall:

* Prioritize education before advocacy.
* Clearly distinguish verified facts from interpretation.
* Present primary sources whenever possible.
* Explain legal concepts in plain language before introducing technical terminology.
* Encourage thoughtful civic participation.
* Respect differing viewpoints while grounding discussions in documented evidence.
* Continuously expand as new research, legislation, court decisions, and reform efforts emerge.

---

# 6. The Civic Learning Model

Every major topic follows a consistent educational structure:

| Level | Format |
|-------|--------|
| **Level One** | One-minute overview |
| **Level Two** | Five-minute explanation |
| **Level Three** | Comprehensive educational article |
| **Level Four** | Research archive — sources, opinions, data, citations |

Readers choose their own learning depth.

---

# 7. Civic Action Philosophy

Education is the beginning—not the endpoint.

As visitors gain understanding, the platform provides opportunities to participate in constructive civic education through voluntary actions.

These include joining the education network, becoming a local education leader, hosting conversations, participating in research, contributing model legislation ideas, collaborating on ballot initiative concepts, and sharing resources.

The platform exists to empower citizens to educate others, not to pressure them into predetermined actions.

---

# 8. Persistent Action Hub

Throughout the platform, visitors have access to a persistent floating Action Hub — a constant invitation to move from learning toward participation.

Initial Action Hub:

* Become an Education Leader
* Join the Education Network
* Share This Page
* Invite Friends and Family
* Contact List Signup
* Draft a Law
* Ballot Initiative Lab
* Community Ideas
* Ask a Question
* Give Feedback

---

# 9. Leadership Development

Leadership opportunities accommodate different interests and experience levels:

Community educator · Research volunteer · Writer/editor · Data researcher · Public speaker · Event host · Campus organizer · Faith/civic outreach · Legislative research · Ballot initiative researcher

Every pathway begins with education.

---

# 10. Long-Term Vision & Governance

This platform is a living educational institution, not a static website.

**Every feature, page, or technical component must support at least one of:**

1. **Increase public understanding.**
2. **Deepen evidence-based learning.**
3. **Develop community education leaders.**
