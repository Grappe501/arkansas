# Purpose

The **Research Institute** is the engine that powers Citizens United Facts.

Nothing should be published unless it has passed through a structured process of research, evidence collection, verification, review, and documentation.

Every article, lesson, presentation, AI response, and educational resource ultimately begins here. The Research Institute exists to ensure that the institution earns trust through disciplined scholarship.

---

# Research Philosophy

The institution does not begin with conclusions. It begins with questions.

```text
Questions → Research → Evidence → Claims → Educational Resources → Civic Understanding
```

---

# The Research Lifecycle

Every research project follows the same workflow.

```text
Research Question
  ↓
Research Plan
  ↓
Evidence Collection
  ↓
Source Evaluation
  ↓
Claim Development
  ↓
Evidence Verification
  ↓
Editorial Review
  ↓
Publication
  ↓
Monitoring
  ↓
Periodic Review
```

Every stage is documented.

# Research Categories

The Research Institute maintains permanent research programs:

Citizens United · Campaign Finance · Arkansas Government · Federal Government · Constitutional Law · Supreme Court Decisions · Election Administration · Ballot Initiatives · Government Transparency · Civic Participation · Historical Research · Comparative State Research

Each program maintains its own roadmap and backlog.

---

# Evidence Ledger

Every factual claim should be traceable. The Evidence Ledger records:

Claim · Supporting evidence · Primary sources · Secondary sources · Date reviewed · Reviewer · Confidence level · Related research

Nothing significant should exist without supporting documentation.

---

# Claims Registry

Each major claim receives:

Claim ID · Plain-language statement · Supporting evidence · Contrary evidence (when applicable) · Status · Review history · Related educational resources

This allows both people and AI to understand how conclusions were reached.

---

# Source Evaluation

Every source is evaluated for:

Authority · Originality · Reliability · Timeliness · Bias considerations · Relevance

Mission Control should record evaluation outcomes.

---

# Research Workspace

Researchers receive tools for:

Managing projects · Organizing evidence · Creating citations · Linking claims · Tracking reviews · Collaborating with peers · Working with AI

The workspace becomes the laboratory of the institution.

---

# AI Research Assistant

Researchers receive AI support for:

Finding related materials · Summarizing lengthy documents · Identifying missing evidence · Comparing sources · Drafting literature reviews · Generating citation suggestions · Highlighting conflicting information

AI accelerates research while leaving judgment to human researchers.

---

# Peer Review

Significant publications should receive review by another qualified contributor whenever practical.

Reviewers examine: Accuracy · Evidence quality · Citation completeness · Clarity · Educational value

Review strengthens institutional confidence.

---

# Research Monitoring

Mission Control tracks:

Active research · Completed research · Research backlog · Claims awaiting verification · Sources awaiting review · Publication pipeline · Research quality indicators

Leadership understands the health of the institution's knowledge engine.

---

# County Research Program

Every Arkansas county should eventually have a dedicated research profile including:

History · Government structure · Major communities · Educational organizations · Libraries · Historical societies · Community resources · Local civic information
