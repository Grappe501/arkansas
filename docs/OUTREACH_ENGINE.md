## Purpose

The Citizens United Facts platform should not rely on people accidentally finding the website.

It should actively—but responsibly—expand civic education across Arkansas by creating multiple pathways for people to discover, learn, share, teach, and invite others into the educational experience.

The Outreach Engine builds awareness while strengthening local conversations rooted in verified information.

The objective is simple:

**Help more Arkansans understand Citizens United.**

---

## Outreach Philosophy

Every outreach effort should follow this sequence:

**Discover** → **Learn** → **Understand** → **Share** → **Discuss** → **Teach** → **Build Community**

The platform exists to help knowledge spread through trusted relationships.

---

## The Five Outreach Pillars

### Pillar One — Digital Education

The website serves as the permanent educational library. Visitors should always be directed back to educational articles, interactive timelines, research library, source documents, and learning paths.

### Pillar Two — Social Media Education

Social media introduces ideas—not replaces learning. Content types: short videos, historical facts, timeline graphics, FAQs, myth-versus-fact posts, Supreme Court excerpts, Arkansas legislative updates, research highlights. Every post encourages continued learning on the website.

### Pillar Three — Community Conversations

Library presentations, civic clubs, neighborhood meetings, community centers, faith discussions, campus organizations, local educational events — all using standardized platform resources.

### Pillar Four — Coalition Outreach

Partners share resources, host events, invite speakers, promote learning paths, introduce participants, and connect organizations.

### Pillar Five — Public Official Education

Easy sharing of educational materials with Arkansas U.S. Representatives, U.S. Senators, and Arkansas General Assembly members — well-organized resources and primary sources for optional review.
