# Purpose

Every action within Citizens United Facts generates information.

Analytics transform that information into **institutional intelligence**. Institutional intelligence helps leadership make better decisions.

Mission Control should answer not only **"What happened?"** but also:

- **Why did it happen?**
- **What is likely to happen next?**
- **What should we do about it?**

---

# Analytics Philosophy

Measure only what helps improve the mission. Avoid collecting data that has no operational value.

Every metric should answer a meaningful question. If a measurement does not improve decisions, it should not be tracked.

---

# Institutional Intelligence Layers

The Analytics Engine consists of five layers.

## Layer 1 — Activity

**What happened?** New volunteers · Course completions · Community conversations · Research publications · Website usage · Organization growth

## Layer 2 — Performance

**How well are we doing?** County progress · Volunteer retention · Research quality · Academy completion · Coalition participation · Community engagement

## Layer 3 — Trends

**How are we changing?** Monthly volunteer growth · County participation trends · Research productivity · Leadership development · Relationship growth · Community health

## Layer 4 — Forecasting

**What is likely to happen?** County readiness · Leadership shortages · Volunteer burnout risk · Recruitment projections · Timeline confidence · Engagement forecasts

## Layer 5 — Recommendations

**What should we do next?** Mission Control provides prioritized recommendations supported by data.

# Governing Principle

An institution should not collect data merely to report it. It should collect information that helps people make wiser decisions, allocate resources more effectively, strengthen communities, and fulfill the mission with greater clarity.

Institutional intelligence exists to **support human judgment—not replace it**.
