# Purpose

**Build #95** provides neutral, factual information about civic participation opportunities — without directing people to support or oppose particular candidates, parties, or policy positions.

> **Educate. Connect. Act.**

Mission Control monitors statewide civic participation opportunities as a public service.

---

# Institutional Philosophy

Learn → Understand → Discuss → Decide → Participate

The institution teaches the process. Individual Arkansans make their own decisions.

---

# The Arkansas Action Center

Answers: Who represents me? When does the legislature meet? How do I contact officials? How do I testify? How does a bill become law? How do ballot initiatives work? Where is election information?

Emphasis: **education and process.**

---

# Civic Participation Library · Action Opportunities · Community Pages

Guides: contacting officials, letters, meetings, public comment, legislation tracking, public records, voter registration, poll worker, boards & commissions.

Calendar: General Assembly, Congress, quorum court, city council, school board, hearings, election deadlines, ballot initiatives, educational events.

Every county/city page: public meetings, local contacts, districts, organizations, volunteer opportunities, educational events.

---

# Legislative Resource Center · Communication Builder · Community Issue Hub

Bill tracker, committee/legislator directories, glossary, calendars, testimony guides, federal process.

Templates: questions for officials, meeting requests, public comment outlines, testimony structure, research citations.

Tracks educational interest in topics — not political positions.

Public meeting calendar · legislative activity · educational participation · volunteer opportunities · county/city activity · community conversations · Action Center usage.

# Governing Principle

> The institution does not tell Arkansans what positions to take. It helps them understand how to engage thoughtfully, respectfully, and effectively.
