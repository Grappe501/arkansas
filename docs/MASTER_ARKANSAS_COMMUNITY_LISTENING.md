# Purpose

Most organizations broadcast. Great institutions listen.

If Citizens United Facts is to become Arkansas's trusted civic education institution, it must continuously listen to the people it serves.

The **Community Intelligence & Listening System** collects questions, misunderstandings, educational needs, community concerns, and suggestions from Arkansans — and uses that information to improve the institution.

The institution should not simply teach Arkansas. **It should learn from Arkansas.**

Mission Control considers **listening as important as publishing**.

# Institutional Philosophy

> **We do not begin with answers. We begin by listening.**

Research tells us what is documented. Communities tell us what still needs to be understood. Both are necessary.

---

# The Arkansas Listening Network — Six Sources

## Source One — Individual Learners

Questions through website, AI Guide, community conversations, Academy, educational events. Mission Control identifies recurring questions.

## Source Two — Education Leaders

Leaders report: questions people ask · areas of confusion · successful teaching · resource requests · new educational needs. Leaders are the institution's eyes and ears.

## Source Three — Coalition Organizations

Partners provide feedback on community interests, educational challenges, resource needs, event opportunities, research requests.

## Source Four — Community Conversations

Every conversation generates: questions asked · topics discussed · misunderstood concepts · recommended improvements · future topics. Statewide trends analyzed; privacy respected.

## Source Five — Research Community

Researchers suggest: new studies · updated sources · legal developments · historical materials · data improvements.

## Source Six — Public Feedback

Visitors submit: corrections · questions · suggestions · additional sources · clarification requests. Every submission receives structured review.

# Listening-to-Action Workflow

Question Received → Categorized → Reviewed → Research Assigned → Educational Content Updated → Mission Control Closed

Nothing disappears into a black hole.

---

# Community Pulse Reports

Monthly · Quarterly · Annual reports summarizing questions, needs, educational trends, research priorities, community observations. Guide institutional planning.

# The Arkansas Civic Listening Tour

Education Leaders host listening sessions throughout Arkansas — not to lecture, to learn:

- What remains confusing?
- What resources are missing?
- What would help your community?
- How can the institution better serve Arkansas?

Listening strengthens trust.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Mission Control displays Arkansas's questions — what citizens want to understand, where confusion remains, which counties need resources, which communities ask new questions. The institution evolves because it continually learns.

---

# Governing Principle

Never become an institution that only speaks. Listen just as carefully as you teach.

The best civic education begins with curiosity. Curiosity begins with questions. Questions begin with listening.

Listening is how the institution remains relevant, responsive, and worthy of Arkansas's trust for generations to come.
