# Purpose

No single organization can educate an entire state.

Citizens United Facts should become the **common educational platform** enabling many organizations to work together while maintaining their own identities and missions.

The Coalition & Civic Alliance Network creates a statewide ecosystem of partners committed to improving civic education about *Citizens United*, campaign finance, constitutional government, and civic participation.

The coalition **multiplies educational capacity** — it does not replace existing organizations.

Mission Control monitors coalition health as one of the institution's **highest strategic priorities**.

**Build #61** — Unifies and extends [ACUCOS/ACEI Coalition (#14)](/coalition/), [Relationship OS (#59)](/docs/MASTER_RELATIONSHIP_OS.md), and [Civic Atlas (#58)](/docs/MASTER_CIVIC_ATLAS.md).

# Coalition Mission

Unite Arkansas organizations around one shared commitment:

> **Every Arkansan deserves access to accurate, transparent, evidence-based civic education.**

Organizations may disagree on policy solutions. They can still agree that citizens should understand the facts.

That common ground forms the foundation of the coalition.

---

# Coalition Principles

Every coalition partner agrees to support:

- Evidence-based civic education
- Respectful public dialogue
- Transparent sourcing
- Intellectual honesty
- Constitutional literacy
- Community education
- Local leadership development

Membership does not require agreement on every public policy issue.

# Long-Term Vision

Libraries host exhibits. Community organizations conduct neighborhood discussions. Universities contribute research. Historical societies preserve context. Civic organizations train Education Leaders. Local partners strengthen county networks.

Each organization keeps its identity while contributing to a shared statewide educational mission — Arkansas's largest collaborative civic education network on *Citizens United* and campaign finance.

---

# Governing Principle

Civic education is strongest when communities learn together.

The institution does not seek to replace existing organizations. **It seeks to connect them.**

Every new partnership expands Arkansas's capacity to learn, teach, discuss, and preserve evidence-based civic understanding for future generations.
