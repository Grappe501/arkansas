# Purpose

The Citizens United Facts platform will eventually contain millions of relationships between people, places, organizations, research, educational resources, community conversations, and institutional knowledge.

Traditional search finds documents. The **Knowledge Graph** explains relationships.

This package establishes the institutional knowledge architecture that allows humans, AI, and Mission Control to understand how every part of the institution connects.

# Knowledge Graph Philosophy

Everything is connected. Nothing exists in isolation.

The Knowledge Graph understands relationships between:

People · Organizations · Counties · Cities · Neighborhoods · Court cases · Research papers · Evidence · Claims · Lessons · Courses · Projects · Events · Volunteers · Education Leaders · Community conversations

Mission Control visualizes these relationships in real time.

---

# Core Node Types

Canonical nodes for:

People · Organizations · Locations · Research · Legal authorities · Educational content · Projects · Events · Institutional assets · Policies · Media · AI agents · LocalBrains

Every important object becomes a first-class citizen of the graph.

Blueprint: [/data/knowledge-graph.json](/data/knowledge-graph.json) · [/data/kg-registry.json](/data/kg-registry.json)

---

# Relationship Types

Examples:

| Relationship | Pattern |
|--------------|---------|
| Volunteer geography | Person → Volunteers In → County |
| Leadership | Education Leader → Serves → City |
| Research chain | Research Article → References → Court Case |
| Education | Lesson → Explains → Claim |
| Evidence | Claim → Supported By → Evidence |
| Coalition | Volunteer → Member Of → Organization |
| Geography | Organization → Operates In → County |
| Community | Community Conversation → Occurred In → Neighborhood |

Everything gains context through relationships.

# Semantic Search

Search should understand meaning—not keywords alone.

A search for **"Money in politics"** should also discover:

Campaign finance · Political spending · Independent expenditures · Disclosure · Citizens United · Relevant court decisions · Educational lessons · Related organizations

The system understands concepts, not just words.

---

# Institutional Memory

Every important institutional event becomes searchable:

Leadership decisions · Research revisions · Volunteer milestones · Community conversations · Project completions · Policy updates

Institutional memory becomes permanent.
