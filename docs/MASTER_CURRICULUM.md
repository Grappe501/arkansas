# Purpose

The Citizens United Facts platform should be more than a website.

It should function as a complete educational curriculum that allows anyone—from a curious citizen to a journalist, educator, student, or community leader—to progressively develop a deep understanding of *Citizens United*.

Rather than measuring success by pages read, the platform measures success by knowledge gained.

Mission Control should track curriculum completion alongside content completion.

---

# Educational Philosophy

The curriculum follows one guiding belief:

> **Understanding comes before opinion.**

Visitors should first understand:

* The history.
* The law.
* The constitutional questions.
* The documented impacts.
* The competing viewpoints.
* The available policy options.

Only after completing this educational foundation should the platform invite users to explore civic engagement opportunities.

---

# Curriculum Structure

The curriculum is organized into six learning tiers.

Each tier builds upon the previous one.

---

# Tier 1 — Foundations

Learning Goal

Understand the basic facts.

Core Lessons

* What is Citizens United?
* Why is it important?
* Key vocabulary.
* Basic campaign finance concepts.

Estimated Time

30–45 minutes.

Completion Outcome

Participants can accurately explain the basic facts to another person.

---

# Tier 2 — Historical Development

Learning Goal

Understand how campaign finance law evolved.

Core Lessons

* Early campaign finance history.
* Progressive Era reforms.
* Watergate.
* Federal Election Campaign Act.
* Bipartisan Campaign Reform Act.
* Events leading to the lawsuit.

Estimated Time

2–3 hours.

Completion Outcome

Participants understand that *Citizens United* developed within a broader historical context.

---

# Tier 3 — Constitutional & Legal Analysis

Learning Goal

Understand the legal reasoning.

Core Lessons

* First Amendment.
* Political speech.
* Corporate speech.
* Judicial review.
* Supreme Court precedent.
* Majority opinion.
* Dissenting opinions.

Estimated Time

4–6 hours.

Completion Outcome

Participants can explain the Court's reasoning and the principal legal arguments presented in the case.

---

# Tier 4 — Impact & Data

Learning Goal

Understand what changed after the decision.

Core Lessons

* Campaign spending.
* Independent expenditures.
* Super PACs.
* Disclosure.
* Election trends.
* Empirical research.

Estimated Time

3–5 hours.

Completion Outcome

Participants can interpret major data trends and explain the distinction between documented changes and ongoing debates about their significance.

---

# Tier 5 — Public Discussion

Learning Goal

Understand the range of viewpoints.

Core Lessons

* Arguments supporting the decision.
* Arguments criticizing the decision.
* Academic perspectives.
* Frequently misunderstood claims.
* Media narratives.

Estimated Time

3–4 hours.

Completion Outcome

Participants can summarize multiple perspectives fairly and distinguish between legal analysis, policy preferences, and empirical evidence.

---

# Tier 6 — Arkansas Civic Education

Learning Goal

Apply knowledge within Arkansas.

Core Lessons

* Arkansas legislative process.
* Arkansas ballot initiative process.
* Community conversations.
* Education Leader program.
* Coalition participation.
* Community resource development.

Estimated Time

2–3 hours.

Completion Outcome

Participants are prepared to help educate others using the platform's resources.

# Learning Paths

The curriculum should support multiple guided paths.

## Quick Understanding

Approximately one hour.

Designed for first-time visitors.

---

## Community Educator

Complete curriculum.

Recommended for Education Leaders.

---

## Journalist & Researcher

Focus on primary sources, timelines, and data.

---

## Student

Balanced path emphasizing history, law, and constitutional principles.

---

## Coalition Partner

Focus on educational resources, presentations, and community engagement.

---

## Public Official Resource Path

Designed for legislators and staff.

Focuses on:

* Historical background.
* Constitutional framework.
* Current legal landscape.
* Educational resource packets.
* Research summaries.

The platform should avoid assuming any particular policy position.

Assessments should reinforce learning rather than function as high-stakes tests.

# Governing Principle

The Citizens United Facts curriculum exists to cultivate understanding.

Its goal is not simply to inform people about a Supreme Court decision.

Its goal is to equip Arkansans with the historical knowledge, constitutional understanding, research skills, and educational resources needed to engage thoughtfully with one of the most significant campaign finance decisions in modern American history.
