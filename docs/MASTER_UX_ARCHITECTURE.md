# Citizens United Facts Master User Experience Architecture

**The Institutional Experience Blueprint v1.0**  
**Dashboard:** [/mission-control/ux-architecture.html](/mission-control/ux-architecture.html) · **Registry:** [/data/ux-architecture.json](/data/ux-architecture.json)

---

# Purpose

The first fifty-one builds established **what** we are building.

This build defines **how every visitor should feel** while using it.

Most civic websites overwhelm visitors. Most legal websites intimidate them. Most advocacy websites tell them what to think.

Citizens United Facts should feel completely different.

The experience should make visitors feel welcomed, respected, curious, and empowered to learn.

Extends Build #8 Citizen Journey · Build #47 Visitor Journey · Build #19 Knowledge Atlas · Build #51 Data Architecture.

---

# Experience Philosophy

The platform should feel like entering one of the world's great museums.

You don't feel lost. You don't feel pressured. You feel invited.

Every room teaches you something. Every exhibit connects to another. Every answer naturally creates the next question.

That is the experience we are designing.

---

# The Emotional Journey

Visitors should experience these emotions in order.

**Arrival** — "I've come to the right place."

↓

**Relief** — "This is easier to understand than I expected."

↓

**Curiosity** — "I didn't know that."

↓

**Discovery** — "Now it's starting to make sense."

↓

**Confidence** — "I think I can explain this."

↓

**Ownership** — "I want to keep learning."

↓

**Purpose** — "I want to help others understand."

Every page should move visitors one step through this journey.

---

# First-Time Visitor Experience

Within the first 30 seconds the visitor should understand:

What Citizens United is · Why it matters · Why this website exists · How to begin learning

Nothing else should compete with these goals.

---

# Navigation Philosophy

Navigation should answer questions instead of listing features.

**Learn** · **Research** · **Explore** · **Watch** · **Teach** · **Arkansas** · **Solutions** · **Community** · **Mission Control**

Readers think in questions, not software modules.

---

# Progressive Disclosure

Never overwhelm. Reveal complexity gradually.

One-sentence answer → One-minute explanation → Five-minute lesson → Complete article → Primary sources → Research archive

Every topic should support multiple depths of learning.

---

# The Learning Compass

Every educational page should include a persistent Learning Compass.

It answers: Where am I? · What have I learned? · What comes next? · How deep am I? · Related topics · Estimated reading time · Completion status

Visitors should always know where they are within the larger story.

---

# The Civic Compass

Separate from learning. The Civic Compass answers:

How can I participate? Join Contact Network · Become an Education Leader · Join Coalition · Share this page · Attend an event · Explore legislative resources · Host a conversation

Education and participation stay connected without confusion.

---

# The Floating Action Hub

Present on nearly every page. Small, elegant, never intrusive.

**Ask AI** · **Share** · **Save** · **Print** · **Join** · **Teach** · **Give Feedback** · **Return to Learning Path**

Context changes based on the current page.

---

# Reading Experience

Typography should encourage long reading sessions.

Wide margins · Comfortable spacing · Excellent contrast · Large headings · Readable citations · Minimal distractions

Readers should lose themselves in learning.

---

# Visual Identity

The platform should communicate: **Integrity** · **Scholarship** · **Warmth** · **Curiosity** · **Precision** · **Transparency**

It should never feel like: A campaign website · A law firm · A government portal · A corporate website

Instead it should feel like a **modern civic institution**.

---

# Trust Signals

Every page should quietly reinforce trust.

Review date · Evidence score · Primary sources · Citation count · Related documents · Authoring standards · Version history

Support learning without dominating the page.

---

# Search Experience

Search should feel conversational.

Instead of "No results," offer: Related concepts · Suggested questions · Similar cases · Relevant laws · Learning paths

The platform should always help visitors continue exploring.

---

# Mobile Experience

Mobile should never be a reduced experience.

Thumb navigation · Quick lessons · Offline reading (future) · Audio playback · Shareable graphics · Community events

Many Arkansans will encounter the platform first on their phones.

---

# Accessibility Experience

Accessibility should be invisible because it is built into everything.

Readable layouts · Keyboard support · Captions · Transcripts · Reduced motion · Clear navigation

Accessibility is not a feature. It is part of the institution's character.

---

# Community Experience

Visitors should constantly see evidence that learning is happening across Arkansas.

County activity · Upcoming events · Coalition organizations · Education Leaders · Recently added resources · New research

The institution should feel alive.

---

# Mission Control Experience

Administrators should experience Mission Control as an executive operations center.

Where are we? · What's healthy? · What's behind? · What needs attention? · What should we build next?

Mission Control should reduce cognitive load, not increase it.

---

# Delight Moments

Subtle moments of delight: Learning milestones · Beautiful timeline transitions · Knowledge graph animations · Historical reveals · Progress celebrations · Handwritten archival notes · Interactive document highlights

Memorable without distracting from content.

---

# Institutional Personality

If Citizens United Facts were a person, it would be:

**Patient** · **Thoughtful** · **Curious** · **Well-read** · **Fair** · **Evidence-driven** · **Welcoming**

Never arrogant · Never sensational · Never dismissive · Always ready to teach

---

# Long-Term Vision

A visitor should leave saying:

"I came here because I had a question."

"I stayed because everything connected."

"I trusted it because I could verify it."

"I came back because nowhere else explains it this well."

That experience becomes the institution's reputation.

---

# Governing Principle

The Citizens United Facts platform should not simply present information.

It should create one of the finest civic learning experiences ever built for a single Supreme Court decision.

Every interaction should reduce confusion, increase confidence, encourage curiosity, build trust, and ultimately help Arkansans become better informed citizens through thoughtful, transparent, evidence-based civic education.
