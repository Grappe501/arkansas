# Purpose

**Build #79** creates the **Arkansas Neighborhood Operating System (ANOS)** — the final layer of institutional architecture.

Counties organize. Cities coordinate. **Neighborhoods build trust.**

Mission Control should treat neighborhoods as the **foundation of long-term institutional strength**.

Extends [Neighborhood Organizing (#57)](/mission-control/neighborhood-organizing.html); integrates [ArCOS (#78)](/mission-control/arkansas-city-operating-system.html), [ACOS (#77)](/mission-control/arkansas-county-operating-system.html), [Relational Growth Engine (#69)](/mission-control/relational-organizing-growth-engine.html), and [Citizen Leadership Academy (#68)](/mission-control/citizen-leadership-academy.html).

# Institutional Philosophy

Life happens in neighborhoods. People trust neighbors, friends, coworkers, church members, fellow volunteers, and community leaders. ANOS is designed around these trusted relationships.

---

# Neighborhood Mission

Every participating neighborhood should eventually have:

- Neighborhood Education Leader
- Welcoming place for discussion
- Access to educational materials
- Connections to city and county teams
- Pathway into Community Education Academy
- Connection to broader institution

# Neighborhood Growth Cycle (8 Steps)

One learner → One conversation → Five learners → One Education Leader → Regular conversations → New volunteers → New leaders → Healthy neighborhood learning community

# Neighborhood Health Score (8 Factors)

Leadership · Learning participation · Conversation frequency · Volunteer engagement · Academy participation · Resource usage · Relationship growth · Mentorship

# Integration Chain

ArCOS (#78) → ACOS (#77) → Education Academy → Citizen Action Center → Relationship OS → Civic Atlas → Mission Control

Every neighborhood becomes a connected node. The **200,000 Arkansan strategy** grows through local relationships.

---

# Governing Principle

> **Healthy democracy begins close to home.**

Around kitchen tables. In libraries. At community centers. Among neighbors willing to learn together.

When neighborhoods strengthen, cities strengthen. When cities strengthen, counties strengthen. When counties strengthen, Arkansas strengthens.
