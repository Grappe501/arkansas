# The Three Statewide Objectives

Every operational decision should support one or more of these objectives.

## Objective 1 — Every County

**75 of 75 counties** with active educational leadership.

## Objective 2 — Every Major City

Educational teams established in the **250 largest Arkansas cities**.

## Objective 3 — 200,000 Connected Arkansans

A voluntary network of Arkansans committed to ongoing civic education.

# Five Execution Phases

## Phase One — Build the Core

Launch platform · publish curriculum · complete Mission Control · launch Community Education Academy · recruit founding Education Leaders.

**Success metric:** The institution is operational.

## Phase Two — County Anchors

Recruit at least one Education Leader in every county. **Coverage over density.** Mission Control displays counties without representation.

## Phase Three — City Expansion

Establish Education Leaders in the 250 largest cities. Local visibility · community conversations · partnership development.

## Phase Four — Neighborhood Growth

Develop neighborhood learning networks — living rooms, libraries, coffee shops, community centers. Small-group conversations become the primary growth method.

## Phase Five — Leadership Multiplication

Every Education Leader mentors another. Every County Leader develops future leaders. Every Regional Mentor strengthens multiple counties. The institution becomes self-replicating.

# Long-Term Vision

Fifteen years after launch: every county represented · every major city has active Education Leaders · community conversations weekly across Arkansas · coalition organizations collaborate statewide · more than 200,000 Arkansans voluntarily connected · the platform is part of Arkansas's civic education infrastructure.

# Governing Principle

The institution grows **deliberately** — not through advertising, pressure, or political campaigning.

It grows because trustworthy research produces excellent education, excellent education develops confident leaders, confident leaders strengthen communities, and communities invite others to learn.

One county at a time. One city at a time. One neighborhood at a time. One conversation at a time. One Arkansan at a time.

That is how a lasting civic education institution is built.
