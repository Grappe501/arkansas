# Purpose

Technology alone cannot build an enduring institution. Clear governance creates continuity.

The Executive Office, Program Management Office (PMO), and governance framework ensure that Citizens United Facts remains focused on its mission, accountable to its values, and capable of making consistent decisions as it grows.

The goal is to build an institution that can outlast its founders.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Authority should be:

Clearly defined · Transparent · Documented · Delegated appropriately · Accountable · Mission-driven

The institution avoids unnecessary bureaucracy while ensuring thoughtful stewardship.

---

# Institutional Structure

The operating structure consists of:

Executive Office → Program Management Office (PMO) → Mission Control → Functional Departments → County Leadership → City Leadership → Neighborhood Leadership → Community Volunteers

Every level understands its responsibilities.

---

# Executive Office

The Executive Office provides strategic leadership. Responsibilities include:

Mission stewardship · Strategic planning · Institutional partnerships · Long-term priorities · Major policy decisions · Executive communications · Annual planning

The Executive Office focuses on direction rather than daily operations.

---

# Program Management Office (PMO)

The PMO coordinates execution. Responsibilities include:

Project portfolio management · Milestone tracking · Risk management · Cross-department coordination · Dependency management · Resource planning · Operational reporting

Mission Control receives continuous updates from the PMO.

---

# Mission Control Relationship

Mission Control serves as the operational intelligence center. It provides:

Dashboards · Forecasts · Performance metrics · Alerts · Recommendations · Operational visibility

Mission Control informs decisions but does not make them.

---

# Decision Framework

Institutional decisions follow a consistent process:

Issue identified → Evidence collected → Options evaluated → Stakeholders consulted (when appropriate) → Decision documented → Implementation planned → Mission Control monitoring → Post-implementation review

Transparency strengthens institutional trust.

---

# Delegation Principles

Decision-making occurs at the lowest appropriate level:

Neighborhood issues → Neighborhood leaders · City issues → City leaders · County issues → County leadership · Statewide strategy → Executive Office

This encourages local ownership while preserving statewide alignment.

---

# Policy Management

Policies include:

Purpose · Scope · Owner · Approval date · Review schedule · Revision history · Related procedures

Every policy has an identifiable lifecycle.

---

# Strategic Planning

Planning occurs on multiple horizons:

Daily operations · Weekly coordination · Monthly reviews · Quarterly planning · Annual strategic planning · Long-term institutional vision

Mission Control supports every planning cycle.

---

# Risk Governance

The PMO maintains an institutional risk register covering:

Technology · Volunteer capacity · Research backlog · Leadership vacancies · Community engagement · Funding · Operational continuity

Mission Control monitors risk trends continuously.

---

# Annual Operating Plan

Each year establishes:

Strategic priorities · Major initiatives · County objectives · Academy objectives · Volunteer goals · Research goals · Technology roadmap

Mission Control converts strategy into measurable execution.

---

# Meeting Framework

Recurring governance meetings include:

Executive strategy · PMO review · Mission Control review · Research leadership · Academy leadership · Volunteer leadership · County leadership · Coalition coordination

Meetings produce documented decisions and action items.

---

# Accountability Framework

Every major objective has:

Responsible owner · Supporting team · Milestones · Success metrics · Review schedule

Mission Control tracks accountability transparently.

---

# Organizational Memory

Every governance decision records:

Decision · Rationale · Participants · Supporting evidence · Implementation date · Review outcome

Institutional wisdom accumulates over time.

---

# Ethics Framework

Leadership commits to:

Integrity · Transparency · Evidence-based decision-making · Respectful collaboration · Responsible stewardship · Service to the mission

Ethics are operational expectations, not merely statements of intent.
