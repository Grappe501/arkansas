# Communications Philosophy

Never communicate to win an argument. Communicate to **increase understanding**.

Every communication should answer:

- What happened?
- Why does it matter?
- What does the evidence show?
- Where can I learn more?
- How can I help educate others?

**Education always comes before persuasion.**

---

# Institutional Voice

Consistently sound: Calm · Respectful · Evidence-based · Patient · Curious · Transparent · Welcoming · Hopeful · Confident without arrogance.

The institution earns trust through consistency.

---

# Five Communication Objectives

1. **Explain** — Help Arkansans understand complex ideas
2. **Connect** — Show relationships between history, law, campaign finance, and Arkansas
3. **Invite** — Encourage continued learning and participation
4. **Equip** — Provide resources that help others teach
5. **Strengthen** — Build lasting relationships with communities across Arkansas

# The Content Pyramid

Research Paper → Comprehensive Article → Lesson → Infographic → Video → Presentation → Social Media Graphic → One-Page Fact Sheet → Shareable Quote

# Editorial Calendar · Social Media · Email · Presentations · Media Response · Storytelling

- **Calendar:** research releases, anniversaries, Court milestones, campaigns, conversations, Academy, coalition, legislative education
- **Social:** introduce ideas, share resources, highlight research, celebrate leaders, promote events, invite deeper learning
- **Email:** educate before asking — welcome, pathways, research, Academy, stories, coalition, MC updates, annual reports
- **Presentations:** standardized materials for leaders — 30/60-min, discussions, library, college, civic org
- **Media response:** verified information, primary sources, historical context, educational resources, respectful tone
- **Storytelling:** historical, Court, Arkansas, community success, leader, research discovery stories
