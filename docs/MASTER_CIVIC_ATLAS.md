# Citizens United Facts Arkansas Civic Intelligence & Community Mapping System

**The Arkansas Civic Atlas — Understanding the State One Community at a Time v1.0**  
**Dashboard:** [/mission-control/civic-atlas.html](/mission-control/civic-atlas.html) · **Registry:** [/data/civic-atlas.json](/data/civic-atlas.json)

---

# Purpose

To educate Arkansas effectively, the institution must first **understand Arkansas**.

The Arkansas Civic Atlas is a living intelligence system that maps communities, civic institutions, educational opportunities, and outreach capacity across the state.

This is **not** a political targeting system.

It is an **educational planning system** that helps Mission Control answer:

- Where are we reaching people?
- Where are we missing people?
- Where should we invest educational resources next?

The Civic Atlas becomes the **geographic intelligence layer** behind the institution.

**Build #58** — Integrates [Statewide Growth (#56)](/docs/MASTER_STATEWIDE_GROWTH.md), [Neighborhood Organizing (#57)](/docs/MASTER_NEIGHBORHOOD_ORGANIZING.md), and [County OS (#31)](/mission-control/county-os.html).

# Vision

Imagine opening a map of Arkansas where every county, city, and community tells a story.

Not a political story. An **educational story**.

You immediately see:

- Where Education Leaders are active
- Where coalition partners exist
- Where community conversations are occurring
- Where libraries are engaged
- Where colleges are participating
- Where civic organizations are helping
- Where no educational infrastructure exists yet

This map becomes one of Mission Control's most important planning tools.

---

# Arkansas Geographic Hierarchy

The Civic Atlas organizes Arkansas using a consistent structure:

**State → Region → County → City → Community → Neighborhood → Education Leader**

Every educational activity connects back to this hierarchy.

---

# Community Profiles

Each participating community should have a living profile.

Possible fields:

- County · City · Population (public data)
- Education Leaders · Coalition partners
- Libraries · Schools and colleges · Community organizations
- Faith communities participating (if they choose to be listed)
- Community conversation history · Educational events
- Resource requests · Educational priorities identified by local participants

These profiles support local planning and relationship building.

---

# Educational Coverage Score

Mission Control should calculate an **Educational Coverage Score** for every county and community.

**Leadership** — Education Leaders present · Leadership depth

**Learning** — Active learners · Curriculum completion

**Community** — Conversations held · Events hosted

**Coalition** — Partner organizations · Organizational diversity

**Resources** — Toolkit usage · Resource downloads

This score measures **educational capacity** — not political activity.

---

# Community Assets Directory

The Atlas maintains an inventory of civic education assets:

- Public libraries · Community colleges · Universities
- Museums · Historical societies · Civic clubs
- Veteran organizations · Community centers
- Nonprofit organizations · Other participating educational institutions

These organizations become potential partners in expanding civic education.

---

# Community Needs Assessment

Mission Control should identify educational opportunities:

- Counties without Education Leaders
- Cities without coalition partners
- Communities requesting presentations
- Areas with limited resource usage
- Communities seeking additional educational materials

This helps prioritize outreach based on **educational need**.

---

# Arkansas Civic Calendar

Every community connects to a statewide calendar showing:

- Educational events · Community conversations
- Academy workshops · Coalition meetings
- Public presentations · Volunteer opportunities

Visitors should browse by county or city.

---

# Community Growth Dashboard

Mission Control should visualize:

- New communities joining · New Education Leaders
- New coalition organizations · Educational events added
- Neighborhood participation · Resource distribution

Growth should be visible **geographically** as well as numerically.

---

# Civic Story Archive

Every participating community can document its educational journey:

- First community conversation · New Education Leader
- Coalition milestones · Local partnerships
- Educational innovations · Community success stories

These stories help others learn what works and strengthen statewide collaboration.

---

# Resource Allocation Engine

The Civic Atlas helps answer practical questions:

- Where should the next workshop be held?
- Which counties need additional Education Leaders?
- Which cities have requested presentations?
- Where should new printed materials be distributed?
- Which coalition partners may mentor nearby communities?

The Atlas supports thoughtful planning rather than guesswork.

# Mission Control Integration

Mission Control should include an Arkansas Civic Atlas dashboard displaying:

- County coverage · City coverage · Community coverage
- Neighborhood participation · Education Leader density
- Coalition partner locations · Upcoming events
- Educational Coverage Scores · Growth trends over time

This dashboard becomes the **geographic heartbeat** of the institution.

# Future Expansion

The Civic Atlas should support future capabilities:

- Regional collaboration networks · Traveling educational exhibits
- Mobile learning events · Resource distribution planning
- Historical education trails · Community-generated content (after editorial review)

The architecture remains flexible while preserving its **Arkansas-first** focus.

---

# Long-Term Vision

As the institution matures, the Arkansas Civic Atlas becomes more than a map.

It becomes a **living portrait of civic education** across the state.

Leaders can immediately understand where learning is flourishing, where new partnerships are forming, where additional support is needed, and how communities are connected through shared educational purpose.

---

# Governing Principle

The Arkansas Civic Atlas exists to help the institution serve every part of Arkansas **thoughtfully and equitably**.

Maps should not merely show where people live.

They should help reveal where civic understanding is growing, where opportunities remain, and how trusted local relationships can strengthen evidence-based civic education throughout the state.

Every county. Every city. Every community. Every neighborhood.

Together, they form one connected Arkansas learning network.
