# Purpose

The **Community Education Academy** transforms research into understanding.

Its mission is not simply to provide information, but to develop confident citizens who can explain complex civic topics accurately, respectfully, and in plain language.

Every course, lesson, presentation, discussion guide, and certification originates from the Academy. The Academy is the institution's educational engine.

---

# Educational Philosophy

The Academy teaches people **how to think critically**, not **what to think politically**.

Every course should:

- Begin with questions
- Present evidence
- Explain competing viewpoints fairly when appropriate
- Distinguish fact from interpretation
- Encourage respectful discussion
- Build confidence through understanding

Education comes before participation.

# Academy Structure

The Academy is divided into schools.

## School of Constitutional Foundations

The U.S. Constitution · Separation of powers · Federalism · Individual rights · Judicial review

## School of Campaign Finance

Citizens United · Campaign finance history · Disclosure · Independent expenditures · Political action committees · Public financing

## School of Arkansas Government

State government · Local government · County government · City government · School boards · Ballot initiatives

## School of Civic Participation

Public meetings · Legislative process · Public records · Community organizing · Civic dialogue · Volunteer leadership

## School of Community Leadership

Hosting conversations · Public speaking · Presentation skills · Facilitation · Listening skills · Building trust
