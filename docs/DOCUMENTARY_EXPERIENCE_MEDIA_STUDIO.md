# Citizens United Facts Documentary Experience & Media Studio

**Multimedia Learning Architecture v1.0**  
**Dashboard:** [/mission-control/media-studio.html](/mission-control/media-studio.html) · **Registry:** [/data/media-studio.json](/data/media-studio.json)

---

# Purpose

Some people learn best by reading.

Others learn through listening, watching, interacting, or discussing.

The Documentary Experience transforms the platform from a traditional educational website into a multimedia civic education institution.

Rather than producing isolated videos, every piece of media should become part of the platform's permanent educational curriculum.

Mission Control should treat multimedia production as a core educational pillar.

---

# Vision

Imagine if:

* Ken Burns directed the history.
* PBS built the educational experience.
* A presidential library organized the archives.
* A university created the curriculum.
* An interactive museum designed the exhibits.

That is the standard this platform should strive toward.

Every piece of media should reinforce the platform's credibility and educational mission.

---

# Media Divisions

## Division One — Documentary Films

Feature-length educational documentaries divided into chapters.

Examples:

* Before Citizens United
* The Case
* The Supreme Court
* The Aftermath
* The Continuing Debate
* Arkansas Today

Each chapter connects directly to related lessons, encyclopedia entries, timelines, and primary sources.

---

## Division Two — Short Explainer Videos

Five to ten minute lessons.

Examples:

* What is an Independent Expenditure?
* What is a Super PAC?
* What is Political Speech?
* Understanding the First Amendment.
* Why Watergate Changed Campaign Finance.

Designed for classroom use and community education.

---

## Division Three — Animated Explainers

Use animation to simplify:

* Court procedures
* Legislative processes
* Campaign finance concepts
* Historical timelines
* Money flow diagrams

Animations should emphasize accuracy over visual spectacle.

---

## Division Four — Interactive Interviews

Future interviews may include:

* Legal scholars
* Historians
* Journalists
* Former public officials
* Researchers

Interview pages should include:

* Transcript
* Key takeaways
* Supporting sources
* Related lessons

Readers should be able to verify claims discussed in interviews.

---

## Division Five — Audio Library

Educational audio resources including:

* Lesson narration
* Historical readings
* Interview recordings
* Educational discussions

Audio expands accessibility for users who prefer listening.

---

## Division Six — Infographic Studio

Create reusable educational graphics.

Examples:

* Timeline graphics
* Court decision diagrams
* Campaign finance flowcharts
* Constitutional concept maps
* Before-and-after comparisons

Graphics should be downloadable for community education.

---

## Division Seven — Presentation Studio

A complete library of presentation materials.

Includes:

* Slide decks
* Speaker notes
* Handouts
* Discussion guides
* Audience adaptations

Education Leaders and coalition partners should be able to assemble presentation packages tailored to their audiences.

---

## Division Eight — Classroom Resources

Educational materials designed for:

* High school civics
* Colleges
* Universities
* Adult education
* Libraries
* Community organizations

Resources should clearly identify the intended audience and learning objectives.

# Documentary Structure

Every documentary chapter should include:

1. Opening narrative.
2. Historical context.
3. Evidence presentation.
4. Interviews or commentary.
5. Data visualizations.
6. Primary source highlights.
7. Reflection questions.
8. Related learning paths.

The documentary becomes another entry point into the broader educational platform.

---

# Media Standards

Every multimedia resource should include:

* Transcript.
* Closed captions.
* Source citations.
* Publication date.
* Review date.
* Related lessons.
* Related encyclopedia entries.

Media should meet the same transparency standards as written content.

---

# Community Media Library

Education Leaders should have access to:

* Downloadable presentation videos.
* Introductory clips.
* Discussion starters.
* Promotional materials.
* Educational graphics.
* Printable companion guides.

These resources support local civic education throughout Arkansas.

# Accessibility

Every media resource should provide:

* Closed captions.
* Full transcripts.
* Audio descriptions when appropriate.
* Downloadable text versions.
* Accessible playback controls.

Accessibility broadens educational reach.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The Documentary Experience should become the multimedia companion to the Citizens United Facts platform.

A visitor should be able to:

* Read the history.
* Watch the history.
* Hear the history.
* Explore the evidence.
* Teach others using professional-quality educational materials.

The platform becomes both a research institution and a public education media center.

---

# Governing Principle

The Citizens United Facts Media Studio exists to make complex civic education accessible without sacrificing depth or accuracy.

Every documentary, animation, interview, infographic, presentation, and audio lesson should strengthen public understanding while remaining firmly grounded in transparent evidence and verifiable sources.

The medium may change.

The commitment to trustworthy civic education does not.
