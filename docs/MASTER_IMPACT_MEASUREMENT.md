# Purpose

Most organizations measure activity. Citizens United Facts measures **understanding**.

This blueprint defines how the institution evaluates whether it is truly strengthening civic literacy across Arkansas.

Mission Control measures not just what the institution produces, but **what changes because the institution exists**.

**Build #67** — Integrates [Arkansas Action Network (#64)](/docs/MASTER_ARKANSAS_ACTION_NETWORK.md), [Civic Atlas (#58)](/docs/MASTER_CIVIC_ATLAS.md), [Civic Intelligence Command Center (#65)](/docs/MASTER_CIVIC_INTELLIGENCE_COMMAND_CENTER.md), [Evidence Ledger (#41)](/mission-control/evidence-ledger.html), and [Sustainability Stewardship (#66)](/docs/MASTER_SUSTAINABILITY_STEWARDSHIP.md).

---

# Core Philosophy

Do not celebrate website traffic, social impressions, or page views unless they contribute to deeper civic understanding.

Ask:

> **"Are more Arkansans able to understand, explain, and teach the subject than before?"**

That is the primary measure of success.

---

# The Five Impact Pillars

| Pillar | Question | Primary KPI |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 **Knowledge Impact** | Are people learning? | Growth in completed educational journeys |
| 2 **Leadership Impact** | Are we developing educators? | Growth in active educational leadership |
| 3 **Community Impact** | Is learning spreading into communities? | Growth in local civic education activity |
| 4 **Institutional Impact** | Is the institution becoming stronger? | Institutional maturity |
| 5 **Arkansas Impact** | Is statewide capacity improving? | Educational reach across Arkansas |

# County & City Scorecards

Each county: Education Leaders · Academy graduates · conversations · coalition orgs · events · participant growth · resource usage · coverage score

Compare progress **over time** — not between communities.

# Long-Term Vision

Twenty years after launch: demonstrable improvements in Arkansas's civic education infrastructure — more people understand, teach, participate; more organizations collaborate; more counties have local leadership.

# Governing Principle

Success is measured by the **quality of civic understanding** the institution helps create.

The institution succeeds when ordinary Arkansans leave better equipped to understand, explain, discuss, and teach one of the most important constitutional decisions of the modern era.

**That is the impact worth measuring.**
