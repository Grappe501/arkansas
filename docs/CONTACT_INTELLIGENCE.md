# Contact Intelligence & Community Relationship Architecture

**Arkansas Community Intelligence System v1.0** · **Build #24**  
**Dashboard:** [/mission-control/contact-intelligence.html](/mission-control/contact-intelligence.html) · **Registry:** [/data/contact-intelligence.json](/data/contact-intelligence.json)

---

## Purpose

The Citizens United Facts platform should do more than collect names.

It should help build an Arkansas network of informed citizens, organizations, researchers, educators, and community leaders while preserving trust and respecting privacy.

Every relationship should exist to strengthen civic education.

The objective is not to build the largest contact database.

The objective is to build the most useful educational relationship network in Arkansas around understanding *Citizens United*.

---

## Relationship Philosophy

Every person is more than a contact.

Every person has:

* Interests
* Knowledge
* Relationships
* Organizations
* Skills
* Geographic connections
* Educational goals

The system should understand those relationships without becoming intrusive.

---

## Individual Profile

Every participant receives a **Civic Education Profile**.

### Core information

* Name
* Preferred contact information
* City
* County
* ZIP Code
* Preferred communication method
* Areas of interest
* Educational goals
* Volunteer interests

### Optional information

* Organization affiliations
* Speaking experience
* Research interests
* Presentation experience
* Professional background
* Topics they would like to learn more about

* Audience type
* County
* Attendance
* Resources used
* Questions asked

## Referral Intelligence

Relational organizing should focus on education.

Track:

* Invitations sent
* Invitations accepted
* Educational resources shared
* Community conversations initiated
* Organization referrals

Mission Control should measure educational growth rather than competitive performance.

## Public Official Engagement

When educational resources are shared with public officials, the system may record:

* Office
* Resource shared
* Date
* Follow-up requested
* Response received (if any)

This creates an institutional record of educational outreach without implying endorsement or lobbying outcomes.

## Privacy & Trust

The platform should adopt a privacy-first approach.

Principles include:

* Collect only information that supports the educational mission.
* Clearly distinguish public profile information from private administrative data.
* Allow participants to update or remove their information.
* Never sell participant data.
* Explain how information is used in plain language.

Trust is one of the platform's most valuable assets.

Its purpose is to help people find one another, learn together, share reliable educational resources, and strengthen civic understanding across Arkansas—one conversation, one organization, and one county at a time.
