# Citizens United Facts Master Evidence Ledger & Claims Registry

**Institutional Truth Architecture v1.0**  
**Dashboard:** [/mission-control/evidence-ledger.html](/mission-control/evidence-ledger.html) · **Registry:** [/data/evidence-ledger.json](/data/evidence-ledger.json)

---

# Purpose

Every sentence on the Citizens United Facts platform should be traceable.

Not just every page.

Not just every article.

Every individual claim.

This build creates the Master Evidence Ledger, the system that connects every factual statement to its supporting evidence, review history, and educational context.

The Evidence Ledger becomes the foundation of the institution's credibility.

Mission Control should treat the Evidence Ledger as one of the highest-priority systems in the entire platform.

---

# Core Philosophy

The platform adopts one institutional standard:

> **Every meaningful factual claim can be traced back to evidence.**

Readers should never have to wonder:

* Where did this information come from?
* Is it still accurate?
* Has it been reviewed recently?
* What is the original source?

The platform should answer those questions automatically.

---

# The Claim Registry

Every factual statement receives its own permanent Claim ID.

Example:

CLAIM-000001

Each claim exists independently from articles.

This allows one verified claim to be reused across dozens of educational resources without duplication.

---

# Claim Record Structure

Each claim should contain:

* Claim ID
* Claim statement
* Plain-language explanation
* Expanded explanation
* Topic category
* Date created
* Date last reviewed
* Current review status
* Evidence strength
* Related lessons
* Related encyclopedia entries
* Related timelines
* Related charts

The claim becomes the smallest verified unit of knowledge.

---

# Evidence Linking

Each claim should connect directly to:

* Primary Sources
* Court Opinions
* Statutes
* Government Reports
* Academic Research
* Historical Records
* Verified Data Sets
* Supporting Educational Resources

Nothing should exist without a documented foundation.

---

# Evidence Strength Levels

Every claim receives an evidence classification.

## Level A

Directly supported by primary legal authority.

Examples:

* Supreme Court opinions.
* Statutes.
* Constitutional text.

## Level B

Supported by official government publications or public records.

## Level C

Supported by peer-reviewed academic literature.

## Level D

Supported by multiple reliable secondary sources.

## Level E

Educational synthesis requiring context.

These entries should clearly identify how the synthesis was developed.

---

# Review Workflow

Every claim follows the same lifecycle.

Draft → Source Collection → Verification → Editorial Review → Publication → Scheduled Review → Revision (if necessary)

Mission Control should track every stage.

---

# Change History

Every modification creates a permanent audit record.

The ledger records:

* What changed.
* Why it changed.
* Who reviewed it.
* Supporting evidence added.
* Sources removed.
* Date of change.

Readers should be able to understand how knowledge has evolved.

---

# Contradictory Evidence

Occasionally, reliable sources may disagree.

Rather than hiding disagreement, the platform should:

* Describe the differing evidence.
* Explain the nature of the disagreement.
* Identify the strongest available support.
* Encourage readers to consult the primary materials.

Transparency strengthens educational integrity.

# Educational Context

Every claim should answer:

* Why does this matter?
* How does it fit into the broader story?
* What should the reader learn next?

Evidence without context has limited educational value.

# Future AI Integration

Because every factual statement has its own structured record, future AI tools can:

* Cite evidence precisely.
* Recommend additional sources.
* Detect outdated claims.
* Identify unsupported statements.
* Build educational summaries grounded in verified information.

The AI becomes more reliable because it reasons from a curated evidence base.

---

# Long-Term Vision

As the platform matures, the Evidence Ledger becomes one of its defining assets.

Rather than asking visitors to trust the institution's conclusions, the platform invites them to explore the chain of evidence supporting each important claim.

Over time, the platform develops a reputation for careful documentation, thoughtful revision, and transparent scholarship.

---

# Governing Principle

The Citizens United Facts platform should never rely on institutional authority alone.

Its authority should come from the quality of its evidence, the transparency of its review process, and its willingness to show readers exactly how every important factual claim is supported.

In the long run, the Evidence Ledger becomes the platform's strongest guarantee that civic education remains accurate, accountable, and worthy of public confidence.
