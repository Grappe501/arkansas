# Purpose

Relationships are the institution's most valuable asset.

Research creates knowledge. Education develops leaders. **Relationships sustain the institution.**

The **Relationship Intelligence System** is the permanent record of how people, organizations, communities, and ideas connect across Arkansas.

Its purpose is not simply to store contacts. Its purpose is to **strengthen human relationships over time**.

---

# Relationship Philosophy

The institution should remember people — not merely their names.

It should remember:

- How we met
- Shared interests
- Volunteer history
- Community involvement
- Organizations
- Skills
- Conversations
- Educational progress

Relationships become institutional knowledge.

---

# Relationship Architecture

Every relationship becomes a first-class object.

Examples include:

- Person ↔ Person
- Person ↔ Organization
- Person ↔ County · City · Neighborhood
- Person ↔ Education Leader
- Volunteer ↔ Mentor
- Researcher ↔ Project
- Organization ↔ Organization
- Community ↔ Event

The Knowledge Graph preserves every meaningful connection.

# Contact Profiles

Every contact includes:

Basic information · Counties · Cities · Neighborhoods · Organizations · Volunteer interests · Skills · Education progress · Conversation history · Meeting history · Community participation · Communication preferences · Relationship strength · Last meaningful interaction

The goal is thoughtful follow-up, not merely recordkeeping.
