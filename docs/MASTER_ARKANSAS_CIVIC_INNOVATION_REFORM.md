# Purpose

One of the most valuable contributions Citizens United Facts can make is helping Arkansans understand **what options exist** for addressing campaign finance, transparency, and civic participation.

**Build #74** establishes the **Civic Innovation & Reform Center** — not to promote a single solution, but to become Arkansas's most comprehensive educational library of civic reform ideas.

Mission Control treats this as one of the institution's **signature educational resources**.

Connects [Research Institute (#73)](/mission-control/arkansas-research-institute.html), [Evidence Ledger (#41)](/mission-control/evidence-ledger.html), [Campaign Finance Observatory (#63)](/mission-control/campaign-finance-observatory.html), and [Citizen Action Center (#62)](/mission-control/citizen-action-center.html).

# Mission

Build the most complete educational collection in Arkansas explaining: what reforms have been proposed · why · who supports/opposes · constitutional considerations · advantages · disadvantages · historical experience · implementation.

**The Center explains ideas. Citizens decide what they support.**

---

# Institutional Philosophy — Six Questions

Every reform proposal answers:

1. What problem is this trying to solve?
2. What exactly is being proposed?
3. How would it work?
4. What constitutional questions arise?
5. What arguments are commonly made in favor?
6. What arguments are commonly made against it?

---

# Eight Reform Libraries

1. **Congressional Actions** — disclosure, reporting, transparency, ethics, election admin, amendment proposals
2. **Arkansas Legislative Ideas** — state disclosure, reporting, education, transparency, ethics; current law vs. future ideas
3. **Ballot Initiative Resource Center** — Arkansas process, history, educational analyses; no institutional policy
4. **Constitutional Amendment Center** — federal and Arkansas processes, historical amendments, finance proposals
5. **Comparative State Reform Library** — transparency, disclosure, public financing, ethics across states
6. **International Models** — comparative education on spending, finance, transparency, oversight
7. **Local Government Innovations** — city/county transparency, open data, engagement, outreach
8. **Citizen Innovation Lab** — visitor submissions enter editorial review; institution remains open to learning

# Governing Principle

Expand the quality of civic conversation. Help Arkansans understand the full landscape of ideas, evaluate evidence, appreciate constitutional constraints, recognize competing viewpoints, and participate thoughtfully.

**An informed citizen is better equipped to evaluate reform than one who has only heard slogans.**
