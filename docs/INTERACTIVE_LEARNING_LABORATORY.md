# Citizens United Facts Interactive Learning Laboratory

**Civic Discovery & Simulation System v1.0**  
**Dashboard:** [/mission-control/learning-lab.html](/mission-control/learning-lab.html) · **Registry:** [/data/learning-laboratory.json](/data/learning-laboratory.json)

---

# Purpose

Reading is only one way people learn.

The Interactive Learning Laboratory transforms complex legal and constitutional concepts into experiences that visitors can explore, manipulate, compare, and understand.

The goal is not entertainment.

The goal is deeper comprehension through interaction.

Visitors should be able to see relationships, test ideas, compare historical periods, and understand the mechanics of campaign finance in ways that static text cannot provide.

Mission Control should measure learning engagement alongside content completion.

---

# Learning Philosophy

People remember far more when they:

* Explore
* Compare
* Build
* Visualize
* Ask questions
* Discover patterns

The Learning Laboratory should encourage curiosity rather than simply deliver information.

# Laboratory One

## Timeline Explorer

Visitors can move through campaign finance history.

Features include:

* Scrollable timeline
* Historical eras
* Supreme Court decisions
* Major legislation
* Election cycles
* Political milestones
* Arkansas-specific events

Selecting any event opens:

* Historical explanation
* Related documents
* Encyclopedia entries
* Educational lessons
* Primary sources

# Accessibility Standards

Every interactive experience should:

* Function with keyboard navigation.
* Include descriptive text alternatives.
* Provide accessible color contrast.
* Respect reduced-motion preferences.
* Offer non-interactive alternatives for readers who prefer text.

Educational accessibility is a core design requirement.

---

# Governing Principle

The Citizens United Facts Interactive Learning Laboratory exists to transform complex civic education into an experience of discovery.

Its purpose is not to simplify the subject beyond recognition, but to make difficult legal, historical, and constitutional ideas understandable through thoughtful interaction, transparent evidence, and guided exploration.

Visitors should leave with a clearer mental model of how the pieces fit together and where they can continue learning.
