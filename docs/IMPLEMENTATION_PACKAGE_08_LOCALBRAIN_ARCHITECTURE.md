# Purpose

Mission Control is the executive brain. The LocalBrains are the departmental brains.

Every major function of Citizens United Facts should have its own dedicated intelligence capable of remembering, organizing, planning, teaching, and assisting volunteers while remaining synchronized with the rest of the institution.

The result is a **distributed intelligence network** rather than one monolithic AI.

---

# Institutional Philosophy

Think of the institution as a healthy organization:

- Each department has experts
- Each expert remembers different things
- Each expert has a calendar
- Each expert has responsibilities
- Each expert communicates with the others

The LocalBrain architecture mirrors this reality.

---

# LocalBrain Definition

A LocalBrain is an independent institutional intelligence that contains:

Memory · Knowledge · Calendar · Projects · Tasks · AI Agents · Reports · Dashboards · Institutional Rules · Department History

Every LocalBrain is responsible for one domain.

# Standard LocalBrain Architecture

Every LocalBrain contains the same core modules.

### Knowledge Vault

Policies · Research · Documents · Templates · Historical decisions · Reference material

The permanent memory of the department.

### Operational Memory

Open work · Past work · Lessons learned · Meeting summaries · Decision history · Volunteer observations

The LocalBrain remembers institutional context.

### Calendar Engine

Meetings · Deadlines · Review schedules · Projects · Volunteer activities · Recurring responsibilities

Every LocalBrain owns its own operational calendar.

### Project Workspace

Active projects · Tasks · Dependencies · Milestones · Risks · Assignments

Every LocalBrain understands its own workload.

### AI Team

Specialized AI agents per domain. Example — Research Brain:

Research Assistant · Citation Assistant · Legal Research Assistant · Source Verification Assistant · Writing Assistant · Review Assistant

Large departments may contain multiple cooperating AI agents.

---

# LocalBrain Communication

Brains communicate using structured messages:

```text
Research Brain → Evidence Brain → Academy Brain → County Brain → Communications Brain → Mission Control
```

Knowledge flows automatically through defined contracts—not direct data manipulation.

---

# Brain Contracts

Every LocalBrain publishes:

Available knowledge · Supported actions · Reports · Metrics · Events · Requests

Other LocalBrains interact through these contracts.

---

# Memory Rules

Each LocalBrain remembers everything relevant and nothing irrelevant.

| Brain | Remembers |
|-------|-----------|
| Research Brain | Citations, sources, reviews |
| Volunteer Brain | Volunteer activity, assignments |
| Calendar Brain | Schedules, deadlines |
| Mission Control | Summaries across all brains |

Memory remains purposeful.

---

# Institutional Calendar Brain

The Calendar Brain aggregates:

Research deadlines · Volunteer meetings · Community conversations · Leadership meetings · Academy schedule · Technology releases · Annual reviews · Executive priorities

Every LocalBrain contributes to one unified institutional calendar (IMP-07).

---

# Relationship Brain

Maintains:

Volunteer relationships · Organization relationships · County relationships · Mentorship · Education Leader connections · Community conversations

The institution understands how people are connected.

---

# Knowledge Synchronization

LocalBrains synchronize through the **Knowledge Platform**. No department maintains conflicting copies of institutional information. The Knowledge Platform remains the single source of truth (IMP-04 Domain 10–11).

---

# AI Safety

Every LocalBrain must:

- Reference institutional knowledge
- Identify uncertainty
- Respect permissions (IMP-05)
- Protect confidential information
- Escalate significant decisions
- Avoid speculation presented as fact

The institution's credibility extends to its AI.

---

# Mission Control Integration

Every LocalBrain reports:

Health · Workload · Projects · Knowledge growth · Calendar status · Volunteer activity · Recommendations

Mission Control observes the entire network (IMP-07).

---

# Executive AI

Mission Control coordinates all LocalBrains through an **Executive AI** that asks:

- Which departments are overloaded?
- Which counties need support?
- What research should be prioritized?
- Which volunteers need mentoring?
- Where are institutional risks increasing?

The Executive AI synthesizes rather than duplicates departmental intelligence.

---

# Personal AI

Every user receives a **Personal AI** that communicates with LocalBrains on behalf of the user:

Retrieve research · Recommend courses · Schedule meetings · Locate volunteers · Summarize county activity · Prepare presentations

The Personal AI becomes the user's guide through the institution (`/ai`).

---

# Brain Lifecycle

Every LocalBrain continuously cycles through:

```text
Observe → Remember → Organize → Recommend → Assist → Learn → Improve
```

The institution becomes progressively more capable over time.

# Governing Principle

A single AI eventually reaches its limits. An institution built on specialized intelligence does not.

Each LocalBrain masters one domain. Mission Control coordinates them all. Together they create an institution that remembers, learns, assists, and serves far beyond the capacity of any single system or individual volunteer.
