## Purpose

The Citizens United Facts platform should never become a static website.

Campaign finance law continues to evolve through court decisions, federal legislation, state legislation, regulatory actions, ballot initiatives, academic research, and election spending trends.

The Research Observatory exists to continuously monitor these developments, evaluate their educational significance, and integrate verified information into the platform.

Mission Control should treat the Research Observatory as the platform's "early warning system."

---

## Mission

To maintain the most current, transparent, and evidence-based educational resource on *Citizens United* and related campaign finance developments available for Arkansas citizens.

The Observatory does not advocate for policy outcomes.

Its role is to monitor, verify, explain, and educate.

---

## Research Divisions

### Division A — Supreme Court Monitor

Tracks new Supreme Court decisions, petitions accepted for review, campaign finance litigation, and constitutional developments.

Outputs: Educational summaries, timeline updates, related lesson updates, source additions.

### Division B — Federal Legislative Monitor

Tracks Congress, campaign finance legislation, disclosure proposals, constitutional amendment proposals, and FEC oversight.

Outputs: Educational explainers, legislative comparison pages, status dashboards, historical tracking.

### Division C — Arkansas Legislative Monitor

Tracks bills introduced, committee activity, floor votes, campaign finance legislation, ethics legislation, and election administration proposals.

Outputs: Arkansas legislative summaries, bill explainers, educational timelines, resource updates.

### Division D — Ballot Initiative Observatory

Tracks ballot initiative activity related to campaign finance and election policy — priority: Arkansas, Montana, Hawaii, and other notable states.

Each initiative receives: plain-language summary, current status, historical context, educational analysis, links to official materials.

### Division E — Academic Research Monitor

Tracks peer-reviewed scholarship, law review articles, university publications, government research, and empirical studies.

Outputs: Research summaries, citation updates, evidence reviews.

### Division F — Campaign Spending Observatory

Tracks independent expenditures, outside spending, Super PAC activity, disclosure trends, and election spending statistics with context and source transparency.

---

## Research Workflow

Every monitored development follows:

1. Identified
2. Collected
3. Verified
4. Reviewed
5. Categorized
6. Educational summary drafted
7. Source documentation completed
8. Mission Control updated
9. Published when ready

---

## Research Priority Levels

**Priority One** — Changes affecting educational accuracy (new SCOTUS rulings, significant federal legislation, Arkansas statutory changes).

**Priority Two** — Major public developments (significant ballot initiatives, large empirical studies, major regulatory actions).

**Priority Three** — Long-term educational improvements (new books, historical discoveries, additional datasets).

Intelligent alerts such as:

* A new campaign finance case accepted by the Supreme Court
* An Arkansas bill related to campaign finance has advanced
* A major academic study has been published

Alerts trigger human review — not automatic publication.

## Relationship to Mission Control

Mission Control uses Observatory data to identify content requiring revision, research priorities, new educational opportunities, emerging Arkansas topics, and areas needing additional primary source documentation.

---

## Governing Principle

The Observatory exists to ensure the platform remains a living educational institution.

Its responsibility is not to react to every headline, but to thoughtfully monitor developments, verify the evidence, and help Arkansans understand how changes in law, policy, and research relate to the continuing story of *Citizens United* and campaign finance in America.
