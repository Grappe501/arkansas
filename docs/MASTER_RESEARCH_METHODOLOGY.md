# Purpose

This document establishes the official research methodology for the Citizens United Facts platform.

It answers a simple but essential question:

**"How do we know what we know?"**

Every educational institution worthy of public trust has a published research methodology.

This platform should be no different.

Mission Control should treat adherence to these standards as a core quality metric.

# Mission

To produce civic education that is:

* Accurate
* Transparent
* Verifiable
* Current
* Understandable
* Consistent

The platform should be judged not by how much information it contains, but by the quality of its research practices.

# Research Principles

The platform adopts ten foundational principles.

## Principle 1 — Primary Sources First

Whenever practical, research should begin with original materials.

Examples include Supreme Court opinions, court filings, federal statutes, Arkansas statutes, constitutional text, government reports, and official election data.

Secondary sources should help explain, not replace, primary evidence.

## Principle 2 — Transparency

Readers should be able to identify where information originated, when it was published, who produced it, and how it was verified.

Transparency is part of the educational experience.

## Principle 3 — Distinguish Facts from Interpretation

Every educational resource should clearly distinguish between documented facts, court holdings, historical interpretation, academic analysis, policy proposals, and questions that remain debated.

## Principle 4 — Multiple Reliable Sources

Important factual claims should, where feasible, be supported by more than one reliable source.

When only a single authoritative source exists (for example, a Supreme Court opinion), that source should be identified clearly.

## Principle 5 — Historical Context

Every major topic should explain what happened before, what happened after, why the event mattered, and how it connects to other developments.

## Principle 6 — Plain Language

Complex legal and constitutional topics should be translated into language that an interested citizen can understand without sacrificing accuracy.

## Principle 7 — Continuous Review

Knowledge changes. Research should be reviewed according to published schedules. Mission Control should identify resources requiring updates.

## Principle 8 — Intellectual Humility

When credible evidence is incomplete, conflicting, or uncertain, the platform should say so. Acknowledging uncertainty increases trust.

## Principle 9 — Educational Neutrality

The platform should explain what happened, what the law says, what the Court decided, what proposals exist, and what evidence supports competing interpretations — without presenting contested questions as though they have only one legitimate perspective.

## Principle 10 — Documentation

Research decisions should leave a documented trail. Future editors should understand why sources were selected, excluded, or revised.

# Research Workflow

Every research project follows the same sequence.

1. Topic identified.
2. Research plan prepared.
3. Primary sources collected.
4. Secondary sources reviewed.
5. Evidence evaluated.
6. Educational outline created.
7. Draft prepared.
8. Fact verification completed.
9. Editorial review completed.
10. Published.
11. Scheduled for future review.

Mission Control tracks every stage.

---

# Source Evaluation Checklist

Every significant source should be evaluated for:

* Authority
* Authenticity
* Publication date
* Relevance
* Completeness
* Potential limitations

The platform should document why sources are trusted.

---

# Citation Standards

Educational resources should consistently identify primary legal authority, government publications, academic scholarship, historical materials, and public datasets.

Citation style should remain consistent throughout the platform.

---

# Research Documentation

Every major project should maintain:

* Research plan
* Source inventory
* Claims ledger
* Review notes
* Publication record
* Revision history

This documentation supports transparency and future maintenance.

---

# Quality Assurance

Before publication, reviewers should confirm:

* Factual accuracy
* Citation completeness
* Contextual accuracy
* Plain-language clarity
* Internal consistency
* Accessibility

Educational quality depends on disciplined review.

---

# Mission Control Research Dashboard

Mission Control should report:

* Research projects underway
* Sources reviewed
* Claims verified
* Articles awaiting review
* Scheduled updates
* Research gaps
* Citation completion
* Editorial workload

The dashboard provides visibility into the health of the research program.

---

# Future Research Capacity

The methodology should support future additions such as:

* Expanded Arkansas content
* New Supreme Court decisions
* Additional datasets
* Comparative state studies
* Multimedia research
* AI-assisted evidence discovery subject to human verification

The methodology remains constant even as the knowledge base grows.

---

# Institutional Responsibility

The Citizens United Facts platform should view itself as a steward of public civic knowledge.

That responsibility requires careful documentation, honest revision, transparent sourcing, respect for evidence, and willingness to correct mistakes.

---

# Governing Principle

The Citizens United Facts Research Methodology exists to ensure that the platform's educational resources remain worthy of public confidence.

Readers should not simply encounter conclusions.

They should be able to understand the process by which those conclusions were reached, examine the supporting evidence, and continue their own learning with confidence that the institution values transparency as highly as accuracy.
