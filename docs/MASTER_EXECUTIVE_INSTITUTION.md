# Purpose

**Build #94** defines how leadership functions, how decisions are made, how departments coordinate, and how accountability is maintained — while remaining an all-volunteer organization.

> **Create an institution organized enough to accomplish extraordinary things while remaining flexible enough for volunteers to enjoy serving.**

Mission Control reflects this governance structure.

---

# Institutional Philosophy

Leadership exists to: serve the mission · support volunteers · protect public trust · strengthen civic education · develop future leaders.

Leadership is not a position of authority. **Leadership is a responsibility.**

---

# The Executive Institution

Executive Office supported by permanent departments. The Executive Office **coordinates rather than controls** — ensuring every department advances the mission.

---

# Executive Leadership Council

Functional responsibilities (not titles of prestige). One volunteer may fill multiple roles temporarily.

Executive Director · Deputy Director · Chief Research Officer · Chief Technology Officer · Chief Education Officer · Chief Community Officer · Chief Coalition Officer · Chief Communications Officer · Chief Operations Officer · Chief Mission Control Officer · Chief Volunteer Officer · Chief Data & AI Officer

---

# Permanent Departments

Research · Education · Technology · Mission Control · Communications · Volunteer Development · Community Engagement · Coalition Development · County Operations · City Operations · Neighborhood Operations · Governance · Finance & Compliance · Project Management Office

Each department maintains its own LocalBrain and operational dashboard.

---

# Executive Meetings

| Rhythm | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| Weekly | Operational review |
| Monthly | Strategic review |
| Quarterly | Institutional review |
| Annually | Mission review |

Mission Control prepares executive briefing materials before every meeting.

---

# Decision Framework

Issue identified → Research completed → Alternatives evaluated → Volunteer input considered → Leadership decision → Mission Control documentation → Implementation → Review

Institutional memory is preserved.

---

# Delegation · Accountability · Coordination

Authority delegated as close as practical to the work. County/city/research matters handled by respective leadership. Mission Control coordinates — does not centralize everything.

Every department maintains: annual goals · quarterly objectives · current projects · performance indicators · documentation.

---

# Leadership Development · Executive Dashboard · Ethics

Every executive prepares future leaders through mentoring, documentation, delegation, knowledge transfer, succession planning.

Mission Control executive dashboard: department health · project completion · volunteer capacity · leadership vacancies · strategic priorities · institutional risks · upcoming milestones · pending decisions.

Executive leaders commit to evidence-based decisions, transparency, respectful collaboration, responsible stewardship, volunteer empowerment, and protection of institutional independence.

---

# Governing Principle

> Leadership exists to make it easier for volunteers to succeed.

When volunteers succeed, communities grow. When communities grow, civic understanding grows. When civic understanding grows, Arkansas becomes stronger.
