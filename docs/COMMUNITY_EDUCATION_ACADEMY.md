## Purpose

The long-term success of Citizens United Facts will not be measured by website traffic.

It will be measured by how many Arkansans become capable of accurately explaining *Citizens United* to others.

The Community Education Academy exists to develop those educators.

This is not an advocacy training program.

It is a civic education academy designed to help volunteers understand complex issues, communicate accurately, facilitate respectful conversations, and build confidence through knowledge.

Mission Control should treat the Academy as one of the platform's highest-impact programs.

---

## Academy Mission

To develop a statewide network of trusted Arkansas community educators who can explain *Citizens United* using verified facts, primary sources, historical context, and respectful dialogue.

The Academy prepares participants to teach, not simply to repeat information.

---

## Learning Philosophy

Participants move through four stages:

### Stage One — Learn

Understand the history, law, and facts.

* What happened?
* Why did it matter?
* What changed?

### Stage Two — Understand

Develop deeper comprehension. Participants learn to distinguish:

* Facts
* Constitutional principles
* Court holdings
* Historical context
* Policy proposals
* Personal opinions

### Stage Three — Communicate

Participants practice explaining complex ideas clearly:

* Explaining difficult legal concepts
* Answering common questions
* Using charts and timelines
* Guiding discussions respectfully
* Working with audiences having different viewpoints

### Stage Four — Lead

Participants begin educating others:

* Hosting community conversations
* Giving presentations
* Working with coalition organizations
* Helping train new Education Leaders
* Contributing to educational resources

## Academy Curriculum

**Module 1** — Understanding *Citizens United*  
**Module 2** — Campaign Finance History  
**Module 3** — The Supreme Court Decision  
**Module 4** — Constitutional Principles  
**Module 5** — Political Spending After 2010  
**Module 6** — Current Reform Discussions  
**Module 7** — Arkansas Civic Education (legislative process, ballot initiatives, community strategies)  
**Module 8** — Teaching Others (adult learning, conversations, presentations, facilitation, disagreement)

## Governing Principle
