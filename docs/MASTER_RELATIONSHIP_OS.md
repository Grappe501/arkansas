# Purpose

Research teaches. Education informs. **Relationships sustain institutions.**

This build creates the **Relationship Operating System (ROS)** — the institutional CRM managing every meaningful relationship within Citizens United Facts.

Unlike a traditional CRM tracking sales or fundraising, this system strengthens **civic education across Arkansas**.

Its purpose: help the institution remember people, organizations, communities, conversations, commitments, and opportunities so that **no relationship is ever lost**.

Mission Control treats ROS as one of its **core intelligence systems**.

**Build #59** — Extends [Contact Intelligence (#24)](/mission-control/contact-intelligence.html), [Civic Atlas (#58)](/docs/MASTER_CIVIC_ATLAS.md), and [Neighborhood Organizing (#57)](/docs/MASTER_NEIGHBORHOOD_ORGANIZING.md).

# Relationship Philosophy

The platform should never reduce someone to "a contact."

Every participant is a **relationship with a story**.

Mission Control should help answer:

- How do we know this person?
- What interests them?
- What have they learned?
- How have they participated?
- What organizations are they connected to?
- How can we continue serving them?

**Relationships—not transactions**—become the foundation of institutional growth.

---

# The Five Relationship Networks

## Network One — Individual Network

Tracks: Learners · Volunteers · Education Leaders · Researchers · Presenters · Community participants

Each individual has a living profile that grows over time.

## Network Two — Organization Network

Tracks: Coalition organizations · Libraries · Colleges · Universities · Community organizations · Civic clubs · Historical societies · Participating nonprofits · Other educational partners

Every organization maintains a relationship history.

## Network Three — Community Network

Tracks: Counties · Cities · Neighborhoods · Community conversations · Educational events · Local partnerships

Communities become active participants rather than static locations.

## Network Four — Public Official Network

Tracks educational relationships with Arkansas U.S. Representatives, U.S. Senators, General Assembly members, and relevant local officials.

Records: resource packets shared · meetings requested · responses received · follow-up opportunities

Purpose: institutional relationship management and civic education — not lobbying scores.

## Network Five — Institutional Network

Tracks: Universities · Researchers · Historians · Constitutional scholars · Journalists · Subject-matter experts

These relationships strengthen the platform's educational quality.

# Privacy Framework

- Collect only information supporting the educational mission
- Distinguish private administrative data from public profiles
- Allow participants to manage communication preferences
- Never sell participant information
- Explain data practices in plain language

**Trust remains central to every relationship.**

# Long-Term Vision

The institution should remember far more than names:

Who learned · Who taught · Who asked questions · Who hosted conversations · Who strengthened their county · Who built coalition partnerships · Who contributed research · Who mentored others.

These relationships become part of the institution's history.

# Governing Principle

The institution is ultimately built by **people**.

Technology helps organize relationships. Mission Control helps understand them.

But trust, education, curiosity, generosity, and shared commitment sustain them.

The Relationship Operating System exists so that **no person, organization, community, or contribution is forgotten** as Arkansas builds a stronger culture of civic education.
