# Citizens United Facts Content Production Factory

**Editorial Operating System v1.0** · **Build #27**  
**Dashboard:** [/mission-control/content-factory.html](/mission-control/content-factory.html) · **Registry:** [/data/content-production-factory.json](/data/content-production-factory.json)

---

## Purpose

The Citizens United Facts platform will eventually contain hundreds of educational pages, timelines, charts, videos, FAQs, source summaries, and teaching resources.

To maintain consistency and quality, every piece of content should move through the same structured production system.

The goal is to make the platform read as though it was written by one institution rather than by many individual contributors.

Mission Control should monitor every stage of content production.

---

## Editorial Mission

Every piece of content should accomplish three objectives:

1. Increase understanding.
2. Increase trust.
3. Encourage continued learning.

No content should exist simply to increase website traffic.

---

## Content Types

The platform should support standardized templates for:

### Educational Articles

Examples: What is Citizens United? · What is a Super PAC? · Understanding Independent Expenditures

### Historical Articles

Examples: Watergate · The Tillman Act · The Bipartisan Campaign Reform Act

### Supreme Court Case Guides

Every major case receives a standard format: Background · Facts · Questions Presented · Holding · Majority Opinion · Dissents · Long-Term Impact

### Constitutional Explainers

Topics include: Political Speech · Corporate Speech · Judicial Review · Strict Scrutiny

### Data Stories

Examples: Political spending trends · Independent expenditures · Disclosure requirements — narrative with visualizations.

### FAQ Articles

Examples: Did Citizens United create Super PACs? · Can corporations contribute directly to candidates? · What is dark money?

### Arkansas Education Guides

Examples: How to host a community conversation · Understanding the Arkansas legislative process · Arkansas ballot initiative basics

---

## Standard Article Structure

Every major article should follow a consistent pattern:

1. Plain-language summary
2. Why it matters
3. Historical context
4. Detailed explanation
5. Supporting evidence
6. Common misconceptions
7. Frequently asked questions
8. Related topics
9. Primary sources
10. Community education suggestions

Readers always know what to expect.

---

## Reading Levels

Important articles should be available at multiple levels:

**Level 1** — One-minute explanation  
**Level 2** — Five-minute explanation  
**Level 3** — Full educational article  
**Level 4** — Research and primary sources

Visitors decide how deeply they wish to explore.

---

## Editorial Workflow

Every content item moves through a common workflow:

1. Topic Approved
2. Research Assigned
3. Source Collection
4. Outline Complete
5. First Draft
6. Fact Verification
7. Citation Review
8. Editorial Review
9. Accessibility Review
10. Published
11. Scheduled Review

Mission Control should display workflow status for every article.

---

## Source Requirements

Every article should identify:

* Primary sources used
* Government sources used
* Academic sources used
* Supporting journalism when appropriate
* Last verification date

Transparency builds confidence.

---

## Visual Requirements

Every major article should include at least one supporting visual when it improves understanding.

Possible visuals: Timelines · Charts · Infographics · Flow diagrams · Comparison tables · Maps

Visuals should teach, not decorate.

---

## Internal Linking

Every article should naturally connect to:

* Related history
* Relevant Supreme Court cases
* Constitutional principles
* Data
* Source documents
* Community education resources

Readers should never reach a dead end.

---

## Educational Quality Standards

Before publication, every article should answer:

* Is it understandable?
* Is it accurate?
* Are the sources visible?
* Does it distinguish fact from interpretation?
* Does it explain why the topic matters?
* Does it help readers continue learning?

If any answer is "no," the article returns for revision.

## Content Dashboard

Mission Control should monitor:

* Planned articles
* Articles in research
* Articles being written
* Articles under review
* Published articles
* Articles needing updates
* Citation completeness
* Reading-level coverage
* Visual coverage

This dashboard provides a live view of editorial progress.

---

## Evergreen Review

Educational content should remain current.

Suggested review intervals:

* Core constitutional topics: every 24 months
* Historical articles: every 36 months
* Current legal developments: at least annually
* Arkansas legislative content: after each regular legislative session
* Spending data: when updated datasets become available

Mission Control should automatically identify content due for review.

---

## Institutional Voice

Every article should sound as though it comes from one trusted educational institution.

Characteristics: Calm · Clear · Respectful · Evidence-based · Accessible · Thorough · Transparent

The platform's credibility depends on consistency.

---

## Governing Principle

The Citizens United Facts platform should never chase attention.

Its purpose is to build a permanent educational library that helps Arkansans understand one of the most consequential Supreme Court decisions in modern American history.

Every article should become a resource that teachers, students, journalists, community leaders, and ordinary citizens can confidently return to for years to come.
