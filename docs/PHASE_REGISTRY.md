## Purpose

The Master Phase Registry is the project's governing roadmap.

Every feature, page, research task, visual, dataset, workflow, and deployment activity belongs to exactly one phase.

Each phase has a clear mission, measurable deliverables, completion criteria, and defined dependencies.

The goal is to ensure the platform grows in a logical, auditable sequence without losing sight of the overall educational mission.

---

## Guiding Principle

Every phase should answer one overarching question:

> **Does this help an ordinary citizen understand *Citizens United* more deeply and, if they choose, become equipped to educate others?**

If the answer is no, the work does not belong in this project.

## The 15 Phases

### Phase 1 — Foundation & Governance

**Mission:** Establish the project's identity, purpose, standards, governance, and operating rules.

**Deliverables:** Constitution · Mission Statement · Vision · Editorial Standards · Mission Control · Master Build Plan · Master Phase Registry

**Completion criteria:** The project has a governing framework that all future work follows.

---

### Phase 2 — Information Architecture

**Mission:** Design the complete structure of the website before writing large volumes of content.

**Deliverables:** Site map · Navigation · Page taxonomy · URL strategy · Drill-down architecture · Search · Cross-linking · Reader pathways

**Completion criteria:** Every page has a defined place in the system.

---

### Phase 3 — Historical Foundation

**Mission:** Build the historical narrative leading to *Citizens United*.

**Deliverables:** Early campaign finance history · Progressive Era reforms · Watergate · Major legislation · Supreme Court timeline · Historical articles · Interactive chronology

**Completion criteria:** Readers understand the historical path to the 2010 decision.

---

### Phase 4 — The Case

**Mission:** Explain *Citizens United v. FEC* thoroughly and accurately.

**Deliverables:** Case narrative · Timeline · Parties · Legal issues · Oral arguments · Majority opinion · Dissents · Key quotations · Glossary

**Completion criteria:** Readers can accurately explain the case to someone else.

---

### Phase 5 — Constitutional Principles

**Mission:** Teach the constitutional concepts underlying the decision.

**Deliverables:** First Amendment · Political speech · Corporate speech · Strict scrutiny · Judicial review · Precedents · Glossary

**Completion criteria:** Readers understand the legal reasoning without needing legal training.

---

### Phase 6 — Impact Analysis

**Mission:** Document measurable changes after 2010.

**Deliverables:** Spending analysis · Super PAC growth · Independent expenditures · Dark money · Donor concentration · Election examples · State impacts

**Completion criteria:** The platform clearly distinguishes documented changes from interpretation.

---

### Phase 7 — Data & Visualization

**Mission:** Transform complex information into understandable visual experiences.

**Deliverables:** Timelines · Spending charts · Money-flow diagrams · Interactive comparisons · Election maps · Relationship diagrams · Infographics

**Completion criteria:** Visualizations help readers understand rather than decorate the content.

---

### Phase 8 — Debate & Perspectives

**Mission:** Present major viewpoints fairly and accurately.

**Deliverables:** Supporting arguments · Critical arguments · Academic perspectives · Policy comparisons · Misunderstood claims · Myth vs. fact

**Completion criteria:** Readers can explain both the strongest supporting and opposing arguments.

---

### Phase 9 — Reform & Current Developments

**Mission:** Explain modern responses to *Citizens United*.

**Deliverables:** Amendment proposals · Federal legislation · State legislation · Montana initiative · Hawaii legislation · Other states · Litigation · Future outlook

**Completion criteria:** Readers understand the range of current reform efforts and their legal context.

---

### Phase 10 — Civic Action Platform

**Mission:** Transform education into constructive civic participation.

**Deliverables:** Action Hub · Education signup · Contact network · Relational tools · Share · Community ideas · Model Law · Ballot Lab · Volunteer pathways

**Completion criteria:** Visitors can easily transition from learning to participating in civic education.

---

### Phase 11 — Community Leadership

**Mission:** Equip volunteers to educate others.

**Deliverables:** Conversation guides · Presentation decks · Printables · Discussion questions · Facilitator handbook · Organizer toolkit · Training curriculum

**Completion criteria:** A new volunteer can confidently lead an educational event.

---

### Phase 12 — Technical Platform

**Mission:** Build a fast, secure, maintainable web application.

**Deliverables:** GitHub · Netlify · Responsive UI · Accessibility · Search · Forms · Performance · Security review

**Completion criteria:** The platform is stable, secure, and production-ready.

---

### Phase 13 — Research Library

**Mission:** Create the definitive evidence archive supporting the platform.

**Deliverables:** SCOTUS opinions · Briefs · Oral arguments · FEC reports · Academic papers · Congressional reports · Legislation · Books · Datasets · Multimedia

**Completion criteria:** Every significant factual claim on the site is traceable to reliable sources.

---

### Phase 14 — Quality Assurance

**Mission:** Ensure accuracy, usability, accessibility, and technical quality.

**Deliverables:** Fact-checking · Citation verification · Link validation · Mobile testing · Accessibility audits · Performance testing · User testing · Editorial review

**Completion criteria:** The platform meets defined quality standards before public release.

---

### Phase 15 — Launch & Growth

**Mission:** Launch the platform and sustain continuous improvement.

**Deliverables:** Production deployment · Analytics · Feedback · Content roadmap · Volunteer growth · Research updates · Feature backlog · Version planning

**Completion criteria:** The platform is live, growing, and continuously improving through measured feedback.
