# Purpose

Citizens United Facts should become more than a place that explains history.

**Build #73** establishes Arkansas's leading center for thoughtful, evidence-based research into campaign finance, constitutional governance, transparency, and civic participation.

The Research Institute does **not** advocate predetermined answers. It asks better questions, evaluates evidence, compares policy approaches, and helps Arkansans understand the range of options available to policymakers and citizens.

Mission Control recognizes the Research Institute as one of the institution's **permanent pillars**.

Connects [Evidence Ledger (#41)](/mission-control/evidence-ledger.html), [Research Observatory (#29)](/mission-control/research-observatory.html), [Campaign Finance Observatory (#63)](/mission-control/campaign-finance-observatory.html), [Research Library (#37)](/mission-control/research-library.html), and [Knowledge Graph](/mission-control/knowledge-graph.html).

# Mission

Advance public understanding through rigorous research on: Citizens United · Campaign finance · Constitutional law · Government transparency · Ballot initiatives · Election administration · Civic participation · Public trust in institutions.

Research distinguishes **established facts**, **competing viewpoints**, and **policy proposals**.

---

# Research Philosophy

Every project begins with questions: What changed? What remained the same? What does the evidence show? What do different researchers conclude? What are the strengths and limitations of proposed reforms?

The institution teaches people **how to evaluate evidence** rather than simply accepting conclusions.

---

# Eight Research Divisions

1. **Constitutional Research** — First Amendment, judicial reasoning, Supreme Court precedent, federalism
2. **Campaign Finance Research** — spending, reporting, disclosure, independent expenditures, trends
3. **Arkansas Civic Research** — state law, ballot initiatives, public records, Arkansas-specific education
4. **Comparative Policy Research** — state approaches; comparison, not endorsement
5. **International Comparisons** — democratic nations; educational context
6. **Public Opinion Research** — understanding, misconceptions, civic literacy, curriculum improvement
7. **Policy Innovation Laboratory** — models, legislation, amendments, transparency proposals; facts vs options clearly distinguished
8. **Future Trends Observatory** — AI and elections, digital communication, finance technology, civic ed tech, institutional trust

# Governing Principle

The Research Institute exists to **expand knowledge, not certainty**.

Its responsibility is to illuminate evidence, explain competing ideas fairly, identify unanswered questions, and equip Arkansans to think critically.

The greatest contribution is not telling people what to think. **It is helping them think more deeply.**
