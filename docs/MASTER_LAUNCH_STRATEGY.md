# Purpose

A successful launch is not the day a website goes live.

A successful launch is the beginning of a long-term educational movement.

This blueprint defines how Citizens United Facts should be introduced to Arkansas in carefully planned stages that prioritize quality, trust, feedback, and sustainable growth over publicity.

Mission Control should manage every phase of the rollout.

Extends Build #50 Build Bible · Build #44 Institutional Roadmap · Build #47 Visitor Journey · Build #52 UX Architecture.

---

# Launch Philosophy

Do not ask: **"When can we launch?"**

Instead ask: **"When is the institution ready to earn public trust?"**

The launch should feel deliberate, confident, and well prepared.

---

# The Six Launch Phases

## Phase 0 — Private Development

**Audience:** Project team only.

**Objectives:** Build core architecture · Populate initial content · Test Mission Control · Verify citations · Complete accessibility review · Test every workflow

**Mission Control Indicators:** Engineering readiness · Research readiness · Content readiness · System health

**Public visibility:** None.

## Phase 1 — Trusted Review Circle

**Audience:** Small invitation-only group — attorneys, historians, civic educators, researchers, librarians, trusted coalition representatives.

**Objectives:** Test educational clarity · Identify factual issues · Evaluate navigation · Improve explanations · Review trust signals

Mission Control adds a **Review Feedback** dashboard.

## Phase 2 — Arkansas Pilot

**Audience:** Limited group of Arkansas learners.

**Objectives:** Observe real learning behavior · Test Community Education Academy · Test Contact Network · Test Coalition sign-on · Evaluate community conversations

Mission Control tracks **learning progression** rather than traffic.

## Phase 3 — Public Arkansas Launch

**Audience:** Anyone in Arkansas.

**Objectives:** Introduce platform publicly · Begin statewide outreach · Recruit Education Leaders · Expand coalition · Publish Research Library

Mission Control begins **public reporting** on institutional progress.

## Phase 4 — Institutional Growth

**Objectives:** County pages · Expand Academy · Publish documentaries · Expand encyclopedia · Grow coalition · Increase educational events

The institution shifts from launching to maturing.

## Phase 5 — Long-Term Stewardship

**Objectives:** Maintain research · Update resources · Review evidence · Train Education Leaders · Preserve institutional knowledge · Continue serving Arkansas

The institution becomes self-sustaining.

# Launch Readiness Checklist

Mission Control should not recommend public launch until complete:

Research verified · Core curriculum published · Evidence Ledger operational · Mission Control operational · Accessibility review completed · Privacy policy published · Editorial standards published · Source Library available · Knowledge Graph functioning · Education Leader signup operational · Coalition signup operational · Contact Network operational

The launch is based on **readiness, not deadlines**.

---

# Launch Metrics

Evaluate success through educational indicators:

Learning path completion · Returning learners · Education Leader applications · Coalition organizations joining · Community conversation requests · Toolkit downloads · Source Library usage

Mission success over page views alone.

---

# Communication Strategy

Emphasize consistently: **Education** · **Transparency** · **Historical understanding** · **Constitutional literacy** · **Primary sources** · **Arkansas civic engagement**

Introduced as a public educational resource — not advocacy.

---

# Feedback Architecture

Every phase collects feedback on: Educational clarity · Navigation · Missing topics · Source requests · Accessibility concerns · Suggested improvements

Mission Control categorizes and prioritizes feedback for review.

---

# Arkansas Growth Strategy

Expansion proceeds **county by county**.

Priority: Recruit Education Leaders · Build coalition partnerships · Host community conversations · Develop local resource collections · Support libraries and civic organizations

Mission Control visualizes statewide educational growth.

---

# Annual Relaunch

Celebrate annual renewal: Research updates · New educational resources · State of the Institution report · Academy updates · Coalition recognition · Community education highlights

The platform is a **living institution**.

---

# Success Definition

Launch succeeds when:

Arkansans trust the platform · Educators recommend it · Libraries reference it · Students learn from it · Community organizations use it · Public officials recognize it as reliable

Mission Control shows steady growth in **understanding** rather than short-lived publicity.

# Long-Term Vision

The public launch is not the finish line. It is the opening chapter of an institution that may educate Arkansans for decades.

Every year: More complete · More trustworthy · More connected · More useful · More understandable · More valuable

---

# Governing Principle

Citizens United Facts should never rush to become public.

It should launch only when it reflects the institution we intend to build.

Because first impressions shape trust — and trust becomes the foundation upon which every future lesson, partnership, research project, coalition, and community conversation will be built.

The launch introduces a website. The goal is to establish a **lasting civic education institution**.
