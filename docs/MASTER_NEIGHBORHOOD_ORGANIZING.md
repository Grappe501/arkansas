# Citizens United Facts Arkansas Neighborhood Organizing & Relational Network

**The Last Mile Architecture — Connecting Knowledge to Communities v1.0**

# Purpose

A website cannot change civic understanding by itself.

People learn best from people they already trust.

This build establishes the **Neighborhood Organizing Network**, designed to connect the Citizens United Facts institution with neighborhoods across Arkansas through relational organizing and community education.

The objective is not to build a political machine.

The objective is to build the **largest neighborhood-based civic education network in Arkansas**.

Mission Control should measure neighborhood coverage alongside county and city coverage.

**Build #57** — The last mile. Extends [Statewide Growth (#56)](/docs/MASTER_STATEWIDE_GROWTH.md) Layer 4 and the [Master Master Plan](/docs/MASTER_MASTER_PLAN.md) Community Engine.

---

# Vision

Imagine opening Mission Control and seeing Arkansas illuminated one neighborhood at a time.

Not by political party. Not by ideology. **By civic education.**

Every illuminated area represents people committed to helping neighbors understand one of the most important Supreme Court decisions in modern American history.

# The Four Geographic Layers

The institution organizes Arkansas into four connected levels.

| Layer | Scope | Mission | Target |
|-------|-------|---------|--------|
| 1 | **Statewide** | Coordinate the entire institution | — |
| 2 | **County** | Coordinate county education | 75 county teams |
| 3 | **City** | Coordinate community education | 250 largest cities |
| 4 | **Neighborhood** | Bring civic education directly into communities | Lasting relationships |

Layer 4 is where lasting relationships are built.

---

# Neighborhood Education Leaders

Neighborhood Leaders are the foundation of the institution. Their purpose is simple: **Help neighbors learn.**

Possible activities:

- Sharing educational resources
- Hosting small discussions
- Connecting neighbors with local events
- Introducing interested people to the Community Education Academy
- Helping identify local organizations interested in civic education

They are **educators and connectors**, not campaign managers.

---

# Neighborhood Profiles

Mission Control should maintain a profile for each participating neighborhood.

Possible information:

- General neighborhood name or identifier
- Associated city
- Associated county
- Education Leader(s)
- Community conversations
- Coalition connections
- Educational events
- Resource distribution

**No precise residential information** should be publicly displayed.

---

# Relational Organizing

The platform should encourage growth through trusted relationships:

- Friend invites friend
- Neighbor invites neighbor
- Family member shares learning resources
- Community organization introduces participants
- Education Leader mentors another Education Leader

Growth spreads through existing relationships rather than mass messaging alone.

# The Arkansas Neighbor Goal

The institution's long-term objective is a voluntary statewide network of **200,000 Arkansans** connected to civic education.

Mission Control should visualize progress by:

- County
- City
- Neighborhood
- Region

# Neighborhood Resource Kits

Every Neighborhood Education Leader should have access to:

- Conversation guides
- One-page summaries
- Printable handouts
- QR codes linking to lessons
- Frequently asked questions
- Presentation materials
- Source packets

These materials emphasize learning and respectful discussion.

---

# Community Conversation Model

Neighborhood conversations should remain intentionally small.

Suggested formats:

- Living rooms
- Libraries
- Coffee shops
- Community centers
- Civic organizations
- Faith community meeting spaces

The emphasis is **dialogue, questions, and evidence-based learning**.

# Mentorship Network

Experienced leaders mentor new leaders.

Education Leader → Neighborhood Mentor → City Mentor → County Mentor → Regional Mentor

Knowledge grows through personal relationships.

# Community Health Indicators

Mission Control should monitor:

- Neighborhood activity
- Returning participants
- Educational event frequency
- Resource usage
- Leadership development
- Coalition participation
- Volunteer capacity

These indicators help identify where additional support is needed.

---

# Privacy Principles

Neighborhood organizing should always respect privacy. The platform should:

- Avoid collecting unnecessary personal information
- Allow participants to control what is shared publicly
- Use general geographic identifiers where appropriate
- Clearly explain how information is used

Trust is essential for long-term participation.

# Long-Term Vision

Imagine Arkansas ten years after launch:

- Every county has Education Leaders
- The 250 largest cities have active civic education communities
- Thousands of neighborhood conversations have taken place
- A voluntary network of 200,000 Arkansans is connected through learning, discussion, and trusted relationships

# Governing Principle

The Citizens United Facts institution grows **one trusted relationship at a time**.

The most powerful educational technology is not the website.

It is one informed Arkansan helping another informed Arkansan understand reliable evidence, ask thoughtful questions, and continue the conversation.

That is how lasting civic understanding spreads across a state.
