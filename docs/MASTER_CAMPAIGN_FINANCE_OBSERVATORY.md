# Purpose

After learning about *Citizens United*, visitors often ask:

> **"What actually changed?"**

The **Campaign Finance Data Observatory** answers that question using publicly available information, historical records, documented research, and transparent data visualizations.

This is not simply a collection of charts. It is a **permanent research center** that helps Arkansans understand campaign finance through measurable evidence rather than assumptions.

Mission Control treats the Data Observatory as one of the institution's **flagship educational systems**.

**Build #63** — Integrates [Research Library (#37)](/mission-control/research-library.html), [Evidence Ledger (#41)](/mission-control/evidence-ledger.html), [Knowledge Graph](/mission-control/knowledge-graph.html), [Curriculum](/mission-control/curriculum.html), [Media Studio](/mission-control/media-studio.html), and [Learning Laboratory](/mission-control/learning-lab.html). Distinct from [Research Observatory (#29)](/mission-control/research-observatory.html) — which monitors developments; this Observatory **explains spending data**.

---

# Mission

Help every Arkansan answer:

- How has campaign spending changed over time?
- What trends appear before and after *Citizens United*?
- What types of political spending exist?
- Where does publicly reported campaign finance information come from?
- What are the limitations of the available data?

The Observatory teaches readers how to **interpret campaign finance information responsibly**.

---

# Eight Research Divisions

| # | Division | Focus |
|---|----------|-------|
| 1 | **Historical Spending Explorer** | Long-term trends — election cycles, federal spending, milestones, inflation-adjusted views |
| 2 | **Independent Expenditure Explorer** | IE definitions, reported spending, reporting requirements, major trends, FAQs |
| 3 | **Political Committee Explorer** | Candidate, party, PAC, Super PAC, and other committee structures |
| 4 | **Election Cycle Dashboard** | Compare presidential, midterm, spending, and participation trends |
| 5 | **Arkansas Campaign Finance Center** | State reporting, agencies, laws, public systems, Arkansas history |
| 6 | **Data Visualization Studio** | Timelines, comparative charts, flow diagrams, maps, trend analyses |
| 7 | **Research Notes** | Source, methodology, date range, definitions, limitations, FAQs per dataset |
| 8 | **Frequently Asked Questions** | Evidence-linked answers — spending increases, IEs, data sources, debates |

# Long-Term Vision

The most comprehensive public educational resource in Arkansas for understanding campaign finance through documented evidence.

Visitors leave with confidence reading reports, interpreting trends, recognizing data limits, and asking better questions about money in politics.

---

# Governing Principle

Replace **speculation with understanding**.

The Observatory does not ask visitors to accept conclusions. It equips them to examine publicly available information, understand historical context, evaluate research responsibly, and explore money in politics through transparent evidence.

**The institution teaches citizens how to read the data — not merely how to react to it.**
