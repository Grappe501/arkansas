# Purpose

Software can be copied. Content can be rewritten. Technology will change.

What determines whether Citizens United Facts remains trusted twenty years from now is **governance**.

This document establishes how the institution governs itself, protects its mission, maintains public trust, and ensures continuity beyond its founders.

It is the Constitution of the institution.

Extends Build #2 Project Constitution · complements Build #43 Research Methodology · Build #36 Trust Framework.

---

# Mission Statement

Citizens United Facts exists to provide Arkansans with accurate, transparent, well-documented civic education about *Citizens United v. FEC*, its historical context, constitutional foundations, measurable impacts, and related public policy discussions.

The institution exists to educate. Everything else supports that mission.

---

# Institutional Values

**Truth** · **Transparency** · **Respect** · **Intellectual Curiosity** · **Arkansas First** · **Long-Term Stewardship**

---

# Institutional Responsibilities

Maintain research quality · Maintain source transparency · Correct verified errors · Review content regularly · Protect participant privacy · Document important decisions · Preserve institutional memory

---

# Editorial Independence

Educational content follows published editorial standards. Decisions based on evidence, documentation, historical accuracy, and educational value — not popularity or political pressure.

---

# Correction Policy

Investigate → Correct → Document → Explain → Learn. Corrections strengthen trust.

---

# Governance Structure

Executive Steward · Research Steward · Editorial Steward · Technology Steward · Community Steward · Coalition Steward · Mission Control Steward

One individual may hold multiple roles initially; responsibilities remain clearly defined.

---

# Decision Categories

**Operational** · **Editorial** · **Technical** · **Strategic**

Mission Control records major strategic decisions.

---

# Public Accountability

Publish: mission, editorial standards, research methodology, correction policy, privacy policy, accessibility commitment, governance principles.

---

# Institutional Oath

> We will seek truth through evidence. We will explain complex issues with honesty and clarity. We will acknowledge uncertainty when it exists. We will distinguish documented facts from interpretation. We will preserve transparency in our methods. We will welcome thoughtful questions. We will continue improving the institution for future generations of Arkansans.

---

# Annual Institutional Review

Mission Control generates an Institutional Stewardship Report reviewing mission alignment, research quality, educational impact, coalition development, technical health, accessibility, transparency, and governance improvements.

---

# Governing Principle

A steadfast commitment to evidence-based civic education, transparent scholarship, respectful public dialogue, and long-term service to the people of Arkansas.

Technology may evolve. Leadership may change. The institution's character should remain constant.
