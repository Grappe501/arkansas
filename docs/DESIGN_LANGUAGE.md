---

## Purpose

The platform should **not** look like a campaign site, government portal, or traditional news outlet.

It should communicate **seriousness, credibility, curiosity, and discovery** — a permanent civic institution for public understanding.

---

## Design Identity

Evokes: national museum · presidential library · SCOTUS archive · investigative newsroom · modern university · civic innovation lab.

**Never:** partisan symbolism or campaign aesthetics.

---

## Emotional Journey

Curiosity → Clarity → Discovery → Understanding → Confidence → Purpose → Leadership

---

## Color Philosophy

**Core:** integrity, transparency, stability, knowledge, public service.

**Domain accents** organize (not persuade):

| Domain | Use |
|--------|-----|
| History | Historical context |
| Legal | Legal analysis |
| Data | Visualizations |
| Civic | Action invitations |
| Research | Source library |

---

## Typography

Institutional titles · section headings · summaries · long-form · data labels · citations · callouts · captions.

**Priority:** reading comfort over novelty.

---

## Page Layout (7 layers)

1. Orientation · 2. Summary · 3. Narrative · 4. Visual explanation · 5. Evidence · 6. Related topics · 7. Civic action

---

## Component Library

14 live components with `ds-*` classes — see [/design-system/](/design-system/) showcase.

---

## Data Visualization

Every chart answers: What is measured? Why does it matter? Where did the data come from?

---

## Civic Action Design

Invitations, not pressure. Appear after educational milestones.

---

## Accessibility

Core requirement: contrast, keyboard nav, reduced motion, focus indicators, screen readers.

---

## Trust Signals

Dates, source counts, citations, review status — transparency builds credibility.
