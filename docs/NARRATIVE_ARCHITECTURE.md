# Purpose

Facts educate.

Stories make those facts memorable.

The Citizens United Facts platform should teach through narrative, allowing visitors to understand not only what happened, but why events unfolded the way they did and why the issues remain important today.

The platform should feel less like reading an encyclopedia and more like walking through a carefully curated documentary museum where every room tells part of a larger story.

Mission Control should track narrative completion alongside research completion.

---

# The Master Narrative

The platform tells one overarching story:

> **How did one Supreme Court decision become one of the most influential and debated rulings in modern American campaign finance law, and what does it mean for citizens today?**

Everything on the platform should strengthen the reader's understanding of that central question.

---

# The Eight Narrative Acts

Rather than presenting isolated information, the platform is organized into eight interconnected acts.

---

## Act I — Before the Case

Question:

**What kind of campaign finance system existed before Citizens United?**

Topics include:

* Early American elections
* Progressive Era reforms
* Federal campaign finance laws
* Watergate
* The Federal Election Campaign Act
* The Bipartisan Campaign Reform Act

Reader outcome:

Understand that the case emerged from a long legal and political history.

---

## Act II — The Conflict

Question:

**How did Citizens United reach the Supreme Court?**

Topics include:

* Citizens United
* *Hillary: The Movie*
* FEC enforcement
* Lower court proceedings
* Constitutional questions presented

Reader outcome:

Understand the facts that gave rise to the litigation.

---

## Act III — The Decision

Question:

**What did the Supreme Court actually decide?**

Topics include:

* Oral arguments
* Majority opinion
* Dissenting opinions
* Vote breakdown
* Constitutional reasoning

Reader outcome:

Understand the holding and the reasoning without needing legal training.

---

## Act IV — The Immediate Aftermath

Question:

**What happened next?**

Topics include:

* Public reaction
* Legal consequences
* Development of Super PACs
* Early election cycles
* Changes in campaign finance practices

Reader outcome:

See how the decision influenced subsequent developments.

---

## Act V — The Continuing Debate

Question:

**Why do thoughtful people disagree about the decision?**

Topics include:

* Supporting arguments
* Critical arguments
* Academic perspectives
* Frequently misunderstood issues

Reader outcome:

Recognize the complexity of the debate.

---

## Act VI — Arkansas Today

Question:

**Why does this matter in Arkansas?**

Topics include:

* Arkansas campaign finance context
* Arkansas legislative process
* Ballot initiative process
* Community education opportunities

Reader outcome:

Connect national issues to local civic education.

---

## Act VII — Possible Paths Forward

Question:

**What options are available?**

Topics include:

* Congressional proposals
* Arkansas legislative options
* Ballot initiative concepts
* Transparency measures
* Other reform ideas discussed on the platform

Reader outcome:

Understand the range of proposals without confusing them with current law.

---

## Act VIII — The Next Chapter

Question:

**What role can citizens play?**

Topics include:

* Learning
* Teaching
* Coalition participation
* Community conversations
* Education Leader program
* Research contributions

Reader outcome:

See civic education as an ongoing responsibility rather than the end of the story.

# Narrative Layers

Every major topic should exist at four storytelling depths.

## Layer One

Headline explanation.

Approximately one minute.

---

## Layer Two

Short educational story.

Approximately five minutes.

---

## Layer Three

Complete narrative.

Approximately fifteen to twenty minutes.

---

## Layer Four

Research archive.

Primary sources, citations, legal documents, datasets, and further reading.

Visitors choose how deeply they wish to engage.

---

# Storytelling Standards

Every narrative should answer:

* What happened?
* Who was involved?
* Why did it happen?
* What changed?
* Why should today's reader care?
* Where can this be verified?

Narrative should illuminate evidence, not replace it.

---

# Story Components

Reusable storytelling elements include:

* Historical scene setters
* Timeline moments
* Courtroom moments
* Decision summaries
* Data stories
* Personal impact examples
* Constitutional explainers
* Reflection questions

These components create consistency across the platform.

---

# Visual Storytelling

Every narrative should consider visual support.

Possible elements include:

* Historical timelines
* Courtroom diagrams
* Money flow graphics
* Constitutional relationship maps
* Before-and-after comparisons
* Arkansas legislative process diagrams

Visuals should clarify the story rather than merely decorate it.

---

# Narrative Continuity

Readers should never feel that they are starting over.

Each page should acknowledge:

* What has already been learned.
* Where the current topic fits within the larger narrative.
* Which chapters come next.

The platform should feel like one continuous educational journey.

---

# Mission Control Integration

Mission Control should monitor:

* Narrative acts completed.
* Historical coverage.
* Story continuity.
* Visual storytelling completion.
* Source integration.
* Reading-level coverage.
* Community education adaptations.

This ensures that narrative quality develops alongside factual completeness.

---

# Educational Integrity

Stories should always remain anchored to documented evidence.

Where uncertainty or scholarly disagreement exists, the narrative should explain that openly rather than present speculation as settled fact.

The platform's credibility depends upon maintaining a clear distinction between documented history and interpretation.

---

# Governing Principle

The Citizens United Facts platform should not simply present information.

It should guide readers through a coherent story that begins long before the Supreme Court decision, continues through its legal and historical consequences, and concludes by inviting Arkansans to become informed participants in civic education grounded in transparent evidence.
