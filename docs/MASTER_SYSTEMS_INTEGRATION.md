# Citizens United Facts Master Systems Integration Blueprint

**Institutional Operating Model v1.0**  
**Dashboard:** [/mission-control/systems-integration.html](/mission-control/systems-integration.html) · **Registry:** [/data/systems-integration.json](/data/systems-integration.json)

---

# Purpose

By Build #45, every major subsystem has been designed independently.

This blueprint answers the next question:

**How does everything work together?**

The objective is to ensure that the platform operates as one integrated civic education institution rather than a collection of unrelated features.

Every system should contribute to every other system.

Mission Control becomes the conductor of the entire institution.

---

# Institutional Architecture

The platform consists of twelve primary systems.

Each system both consumes information from and contributes information to the others.

---

# System One — Public Education Platform

**Purpose:** Teach visitors.

**Produces:** Learning activity, resource usage, frequently asked questions, educational progress.

**Consumes:** Research, sources, encyclopedia entries, videos, infographics.

---

# System Two — Encyclopedia

**Purpose:** Provide authoritative reference material.

**Produces:** Encyclopedia entries, definitions, historical summaries.

**Consumes:** Research, Claims Registry, Source Library, Knowledge Graph.

---

# System Three — Source Library

**Purpose:** Preserve evidence.

**Produces:** Source collections, primary documents, citation references.

**Consumes:** Government publications, court opinions, academic research, historical records.

---

# System Four — Evidence Ledger

**Purpose:** Verify every factual claim.

**Produces:** Verified claims, evidence ratings, review history.

**Consumes:** Source Library, Research Observatory, editorial review.

---

# System Five — Research Observatory

**Purpose:** Monitor ongoing developments.

**Produces:** Research updates, legislative monitoring, court monitoring, data updates.

**Consumes:** Government releases, academic publications, public datasets.

---

# System Six — Community Education Academy

**Purpose:** Develop Education Leaders.

**Produces:** Trained volunteers, presentation activity, community conversations.

**Consumes:** Curriculum, videos, presentation resources, research updates.

---

# System Seven — Coalition Network

**Purpose:** Connect Arkansas organizations.

**Produces:** Partnerships, community events, resource distribution, educational reach.

**Consumes:** Academy resources, presentation materials, event support, Mission Control analytics.

---

# System Eight — Civic Action Lab

**Purpose:** Provide educational exploration of legislative and ballot initiative processes.

**Produces:** Educational workspaces, comparative analyses, research questions.

**Consumes:** Research, constitutional analysis, historical materials, Source Library.

---

# System Nine — Media Studio

**Purpose:** Teach through multimedia.

**Produces:** Videos, infographics, podcasts, presentations, documentaries.

**Consumes:** Curriculum, encyclopedia, Source Library, research.

---

# System Ten — Knowledge Graph

**Purpose:** Connect everything.

**Produces:** Learning recommendations, relationship maps, topic navigation.

**Consumes:** Every other system. The Knowledge Graph is the institutional nervous system.

---

# System Eleven — Mission Control

**Purpose:** Monitor institutional health.

**Produces:** Progress dashboards, readiness reports, growth metrics, executive reports.

**Consumes:** Every operational system. Mission Control is the executive cockpit.

---

# System Twelve — Contact & Relationship Network

**Purpose:** Support community education.

**Produces:** Education Leaders, coalition growth, event participation, community conversations.

**Consumes:** Website forms, Academy participation, coalition sign-ons, outreach campaigns.

---

# Information Flow

Research → Evidence Verification → Educational Content → Media → Learning → Community Education → Coalition Growth → Mission Control → Research Priorities → Research

The institution continuously learns and improves.

---

# Executive Intelligence Layer

Mission Control should synthesize information from every system.

Questions include: Which topics need additional research? Which counties need Education Leaders? Which resources are most used? Which questions remain unanswered? Which coalition partners are most active? Which curriculum modules need updating?

---

# Unified User Journey

Visit Homepage → Learn Basic Concepts → Explore Timeline → Read Encyclopedia → View Sources → Watch Documentary → Join Contact Network → Complete Academy → Host Community Conversation → Join Coalition → Contribute Research → Mentor New Education Leaders

Every system supports the next stage of civic learning.

---

# Institutional Memory

Every system contributes to permanent institutional memory: research history, editorial history, build history, coalition history, community education history, Mission Control reports, resource revisions.

---

# Scalability

Because every subsystem communicates through well-defined relationships, future additions can integrate cleanly.

---

# Mission Control Integration

Mission Control should include a Systems Health dashboard with indicators for Research, Evidence, Content, Curriculum, Media, Coalition, County, Academy, Outreach, and Technical health.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Citizens United Facts should function as one coordinated educational institution where every system continuously supports every other system.

---

# Governing Principle

The strength of Citizens United Facts will not come from any single feature.

Its strength will come from the integration of all its systems into one coherent institution where research, education, technology, community engagement, and transparency reinforce one another.

When every system works together, the platform becomes greater than the sum of its parts.
