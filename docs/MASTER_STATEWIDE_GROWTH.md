# Citizens United Facts Statewide Growth & Leadership Blueprint

**Arkansas Civic Education Network — County, City & Neighborhood Expansion Strategy v1.0**  
**Dashboard:** [/mission-control/statewide-growth.html](/mission-control/statewide-growth.html) · **Registry:** [/data/statewide-growth.json](/data/statewide-growth.json)

---

# Purpose

The Citizens United Facts platform is designed to educate individuals.

The long-term institution is designed to build an educational network that reaches every part of Arkansas.

This document establishes the **measurable statewide growth objectives** that Mission Control will monitor from launch forward.

These objectives are not simply participation metrics. They represent the infrastructure required to build a durable civic education movement across Arkansas.

**Build #56** — Expands the institution around measurable statewide objectives. Aligns with the [Master Master Plan](/docs/MASTER_MASTER_PLAN.md) Community Engine and Arkansas Model.

---

# The Arkansas Vision

Every Arkansan should have a reasonable opportunity to learn about *Citizens United* through someone they know or through an organization in their own community.

The institution should grow outward from **trusted local relationships** rather than relying solely on statewide communications.

---

# Statewide Growth Objectives

## Objective One — Education Leaders in All 75 Counties

Every Arkansas county should have at least one trained Education Leader.

**Preferred long-term structure:**

- Minimum: 1 Education Leader
- Target: 3–5 active Education Leaders
- Mature counties: 10 or more, depending on population and activity

Mission Control should display:

- Counties with no leaders
- Counties with one leader
- Counties meeting target
- Counties exceeding target

---

## Objective Two — Education Leaders in the 250 Largest Arkansas Cities

Every major community should have local educational leadership.

Mission Control should maintain a city dashboard showing:

- Education Leaders
- Coalition organizations
- Community conversations
- Educational events
- Resource distribution
- Growth trends

This provides a second layer of local educational coverage beyond county organization.

---

## Objective Three — 200,000 Arkansas Community Participants

The institution's long-term goal is to build a voluntary network of **200,000 Arkansans** who choose to stay connected to civic education.

Participants may choose different levels of involvement:

- Receive educational updates
- Attend community conversations
- Share educational resources
- Participate in surveys
- Volunteer occasionally
- Become an Education Leader
- Join coalition activities

Mission Control should report growth toward this statewide objective.

---

# Neighborhood Network

The platform should encourage participants to identify the general area in which they are willing to help educate others.

The emphasis is **community connection** — not precise location tracking.

Possible participation includes:

- Neighborhood conversations
- Local discussion groups
- Community presentations
- Resource sharing
- Event hosting

**Privacy remains a core design principle.**

---

# Leadership Ladder

Every participant should see a clear pathway for deeper involvement.

Visitor → Subscriber → Learner → Community Participant → Volunteer → Education Leader → County Leader → Regional Mentor

This creates sustainable leadership development.

---

# Geographic Coverage Dashboard

Mission Control should include interactive maps showing:

**County Coverage** — Education Leaders · Coalition partners · Community activity

**City Coverage** — Education Leaders · Events · Organization participation

**Statewide Participation** — Total participants · Growth over time · Regional distribution

These maps help prioritize future outreach.

---

# Community Benchmarks

Mission Control should track progress toward goals such as:

- 75 of 75 counties represented
- 250 largest cities represented
- 200,000 participants connected
- Coalition organizations in every region
- Community conversations occurring statewide

These benchmarks provide long-term direction while allowing incremental progress.

---

# Recruitment Channels

Growth should occur through multiple educational pathways:

- Website signups
- Community presentations
- Coalition organizations
- Libraries
- Civic organizations
- Colleges and universities
- Faith communities
- Social media
- Friend and family referrals (relational organizing)

Every recruitment pathway should lead back to learning resources.

---

# County Growth Plans

Each county should eventually maintain a local growth strategy including:

- Leadership recruitment
- Organization outreach
- Educational events
- Resource distribution
- Community conversation schedule

Mission Control should identify counties needing additional support.

---

# Success Indicators

The institution should evaluate growth through meaningful civic education outcomes:

- Education Leaders trained
- Counties represented
- Cities represented
- Active community participants
- Coalition organizations
- Community conversations held
- Educational resources shared
- Returning learners

These indicators reflect **educational capacity** rather than simple audience size.

---

# Long-Term Vision

When this blueprint reaches maturity:

- Every Arkansas county has trusted local Education Leaders.
- The 250 largest cities have visible civic education activity.
- A statewide network of 200,000 Arkansans is connected through voluntary participation.
- Coalition organizations help sustain local education.
- Citizens United Facts becomes part of Arkansas's civic infrastructure, with learning rooted in neighborhoods, communities, and trusted local relationships.

---

# Governing Principle

The ultimate measure of success is not how many people visit the website.

It is whether every Arkansan has a nearby opportunity to learn, ask questions, access reliable evidence, and participate in thoughtful civic education.

The institution grows one person, one neighborhood, one city, and one county at a time until a durable statewide educational network exists.
