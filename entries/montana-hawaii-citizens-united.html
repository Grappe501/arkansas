Montana & Hawaii: Two Paths Against Citizens United

A verified snapshot comparing Montana's ballot-initiative path (Initiative 194) with Hawaii's legislative test-case path (Act 11) — the right comparison of people-powered democracy versus legislature-enacted corporate-charter reform.

Research Context — Three Tracks

Ernie's research is structured across three parallel tracks. This entry focuses on Track 2 (reform efforts) and Track 3 (ballot initiatives), with Montana and Hawaii as the first verified state comparison.

Track 1 — Citizens United Legal/Political History

  • What the decision actually held.
  • How it enabled Super PAC/independent-expenditure power.
  • What states can and cannot regulate after Citizens United.

Track 2 — Current Anti–Citizens United Reform Efforts

  • Maine is important: voters approved a 2024 measure limiting contributions to Super PACs, and the resulting litigation is now a major test case.
  • American Promise is pushing a federal constitutional amendment to restore authority over campaign-finance rules.

Track 3 — Ballot Initiatives Currently Circulating

State Comparison

Montana

Ballot Initiative Path

Montana Initiative 194

"Exclude Political Spending from Artificial Persons' Charter Powers Initiative" (2026)

Would prohibit "artificial persons" from contributing to campaigns, ballot-measure elections, or political parties.

Status: Signatures submitted to county election officials by the June 2026 deadline. County verification due to the state by July 17, 2026.

Hawaii

Legislative Test-Case Path

Hawaii is not a citizen ballot initiative state in the same way Montana is. Instead, Hawaii enacted legislation using a similar corporate-charter theory.

Act 11 — Signed May 2026

Restricts corporations and other artificial entities from election spending using state corporate law. Takes effect July 1, 2027. Expected to face First Amendment litigation.

Supporters frame Hawaii as a "test state" for undoing Citizens United through state corporate law.

Critics argue the law likely conflicts with Supreme Court precedent.

Sources

Bottom Line

Montana = ballot initiative path. Hawaii = legislative test-case path. These two together are exactly the right comparison: one people-powered ballot route, one legislature-enacted route.
